I never dreamed that taking my first weight training class while at
the university would have culminated in such a rewarding career in
the field of strength and conditioning. It is difficult to thank every
person who had a role in getting me to this point in my life and my
career, which enabled me to enthusiastically embark on this project.
I was fortunate to receive a strong foundation from my parents—I
wish you could both be here to see that the example you set was
followed. I also want to thank my brother and my circle of friends,
both personally and professionally, who have always been supportive
and have been there to brighten my day. While the list of individuals
who have influenced my career is long, my two greatest guides have
been Mike Stone and Bill Kraemer. I value your mentorship and
friendship greatly. Numerous colleagues and former students around
the world have contributed to my knowledge and success along the
way, and I appreciate each and every one of you even if we don’t
see each other very often.
Finally, to my co-editor and good friend for more than 30 years,
Greg Haff: Who would have thought that sitting around at the lunch
buffet as graduate students talking strength and conditioning would
have led to this endeavor….twice? While we have never lived in the
same town, we have supported each other through life’s ups and
downs. I am grateful for you and I look forward to many more years
of friendship and collaboration.
