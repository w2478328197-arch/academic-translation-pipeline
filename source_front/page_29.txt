Essentials of Strength Training and Conditioning is the primary
resource for individuals preparing for the National Strength and
Conditioning Association’s Certified Strength and Conditioning
Specialist (CSCS) certification exam.
As a worldwide authority on strength and conditioning, the
National Strength and Conditioning Association (NSCA) supports and
disseminates research-based knowledge and its practical application
to improve athletic performance and fitness. With over 60,000
members and certified professionals, the NSCA has established itself
as an international clearinghouse for strength and conditioning
research, theories, and practices.
The NSCA offers five distinct certifications that serve the full
spectrum of fitness, performance, and tactical professions. The
Certified Strength and Conditioning Specialist (CSCS) supports
coaches who train athletes and teams by emphasizing scientific
principles, performance testing, and sport-specific program design.
The NSCA Certified Personal Trainer (NSCA-CPT) equips trainers who
work with general-population clients to conduct assessments, plan
individualized programs, and ensure safe, effective exercise
technique. The Certified Special Population Specialist (CSPS) focuses
on professionals who train clients with chronic conditions or unique
health needs, highlighting tailored modifications and safety-focused
programming. The Tactical Strength and Conditioning Facilitator
(TSAC-F) prepares those who support military, law enforcement, and
first-responder personnel by emphasizing physical readiness, injury-
risk reduction, and resilience. Finally, the Certified Performance and
Sport Scientist (CPSS) is designed for practitioners who apply
research, data, and technology to monitor, evaluate, and enhance
athletic performance. All five NSCA credentials follow rigorous
professional standards and require ongoing continuing education to
remain active.
