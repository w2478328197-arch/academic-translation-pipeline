Hold-Relax
The hold-relax technique begins with a passive prestretch that is
held at the point of mild discomfort for 10 seconds (figure 15.3). The
partner then applies a hip flexion force and instructs the athlete,
“Hold and do not let me move the leg.” The athlete holds and resists
the movement so that an isometric muscle action occurs and is held
for 6 seconds (figure 15.4). The athlete then relaxes, and a passive
stretch is performed and held for 30 seconds (figure 15.5). The final
stretch should be of greater magnitude due to autogenic inhibition
(i.e., activation of the hamstrings).
