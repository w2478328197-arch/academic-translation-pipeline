1. Lean in the direction of travel.
2. Explosively hop left over the first hurdle with the right foot,
using both arms to assist.
3. Dorsiflex the ankles in preparation for an active flat-foot
landing.
4. Land on the right foot, and immediately repeat the hop over the
next hurdle.
5. Land on the right foot, and immediately repeat the hop over the
next two hurdles.
6. Land on the left foot, and immediately hop right over the first
hurdle with the left foot, using both arms to assist.
7. Land on the left foot, and immediately repeat the hop over the
next three hurdles; finish by landing on the right foot.
COMMON ERRORS
Not reflecting subsequent takeoff direction in the body
lean (i.e., the center of mass in the takeoff and the
landing before direction change should reflect the
direction of travel in the subsequent movement; failure
to do so increases the ground contact time and reduces
the effectiveness of the drill)
Not increasing ground contact time for both the two-
hurdle hop and the change of direction movement,
meaning insufficient force is produced in the jump
