Formula: DEPTH: actual distance of the foot position (the
farthest foot position if the feet are staggered) from the
anchor point of the suspension strap (measure out from a
spot on the ground directly under the anchor point) + the
athlete’s height + a safety cushion of 3 ft (0.9 m)
multiplied by WIDTH: a suggested user space of 2 ft (0.6
m) for a suspension exercise + a double-wide safety
cushion of 6 ft (1.8 m)
Example 1 (angled use): If a 6 ft tall athlete is doing an
angled suspension exercise (e.g., a suspension row) with
both feet positioned 3 ft from the suspension strap anchor
point: DEPTH: (3 ft [distance out from under the anchor
point] + 6 ft [athlete’s height] + 3 ft [perimeter walkway
safety cushion]) × WIDTH: (2 ft [user space] + 6 ft [safety
cushion]) = 96 ft2
Example 2 (vertical or perpendicular use; metric
approximations): If a 1.6 m tall athlete is doing a prone
suspension exercise (e.g., a foot-elevated knee tuck) with
the feet in the straps directly below the anchor point:
DEPTH: (0 m [distance out from under the anchor point] +
1.6 m [athlete height] + 1 m [safety cushion]) × WIDTH:
(1 m [user space] + 2 m [safety cushion]) ≈ 8 m2
aFor prone and supine ground-based barbell exercises with no bench, use the athlete’s
height for the DEPTH.
bFor dumbbell and kettlebell exercises (i.e., standing exercises without a bar), use a user
space of 2 ft (0.6 m) for the WIDTH.
