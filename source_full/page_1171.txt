Stand facing the axis of rotation of the landmine barbell with a
slight forward lean, while assuming a shoulder-width stance
with the knees slightly flexed and grasping the end of the bar
with one hand.
The bar should be positioned about 4 to 6 inches (10.2-15.2
cm) away from the shoulder of the arm that is holding the bar
(135). The other arm is held out to the side of the body and
used for balance during the exercise.
Downward Movement Phase
Begin the exercise by flexing the hips and knees slowly and
under control while keeping the torso-to-floor angle relatively
constant.
Maintain a flat-foot position with the knees aligned over the feet
as they flex.
Continue flexing the hips and knees until the thighs are parallel
to the floor, the torso begins to round or flex forward, or the
heels begin to rise off the floor.
Upward Movement Phase
Extend the hips and knees while keeping the torso-to-floor
angle relatively constant.
Keep the torso erect, shoulders back, and chest up and out.
Keep the heels on the floor and the knees aligned over the feet.
Continue extending the hips and knees; as the fully-standing
position is reached, press the bar up and slightly forward until
the elbow of the arm holding the bar is fully extended; full knee
and elbow extension should be attained at about the same
time.
Return to the Starting Position
Keeping the lower body and torso stationary, slowly lower the
bar in a controlled manner until it is returned to the starting
position.
