Being where your feet are.… Be in this moment.” When this happens during critical
times during a training session or sport, it is considered clutch. Purposeful focus,
optimal emotional control, and high effort describes clutch performances and is
often unpleasant during the event. Several stress management techniques can be
employed to help work toward flow and clutch, including rhythmic breathing,
attentional cuing, and presence-centered focus. However, the yips and choking
have been linked to the physiological states of fight-or-flight and, in some case,
trauma. Fight-or-flight refers to the physiological response of perception of threat
that readies the body to defend vigorously or run away. Thus, if athletes are
experiencing these issues, professional support is warranted.
