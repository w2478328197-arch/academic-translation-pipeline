Designing an Aerobic Endurance
Program
An effective aerobic endurance training program must include an
exercise prescription specifically developed for the individual athlete
based on his or her individual strengths and weaknesses, goals, and
sport. This requires manipulation of the primary program design
variables. The sidebar lists the design variables as steps 1 through 5.
Unfortunately, strength and conditional professionals and athletes
often use the training practices or programs of current successful
strength and conditional professionals or athletes in their sport. This
does not carefully consider the strengths and weaknesses of the
athlete and may lead to development of an ineffective or potentially
harmful training program. The optimal development of a safe and
effective training program is to have the factors related to aerobic
endurance performance evaluated and then use that information to
generate a training program specific to the athlete. For example, an
athlete with poor exercise economy should place a greater emphasis
on training to improve exercise economy compared to other factors
that are known strengths. This might include HIIT with a focus on
technique, as well as using long rest periods. Conversely, athletes
who need to increase LT might consider performing more high-
intensity training with shorter rest periods.
