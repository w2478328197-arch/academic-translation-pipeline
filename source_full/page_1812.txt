Athletes should wear proper training attire, particularly shirts and athletic
shoes, at all times.
Athletes should use spotters for exercises that place the bar on the back or
front shoulders and exercises that involve a bar or dumbbells moving over
the face or above the head. Power exercises are not spotted.
Athletes should immediately report any facility-related injury or facility or
equipment irregularity to the supervisor on duty.
Tobacco, food, chewing gum, glass bottles, cans, alcohol, drugs, and banned
substances are not allowed in the strength and conditioning facility; plastic
water bottles are acceptable.
Supervisors are not responsible for users’ personal belongings or lost or
stolen items.
Jewelry such as loose necklaces, bracelets, hanging earrings, and watches
should not be worn.
Athletes should keep feet off the walls.
Athletes should minimize chalk and powder on the floor.
All guests and visitors must report to the office to sign the waiver form.
Former athletes must have their programs preapproved by a staff supervisor
and must sign a waiver form.
Athletic department personnel can use these facilities for personal training
sessions if they do not interfere with the needs of the athletes.
Non–athletic department personnel are allowed to use the facilities with
permission of the athletic director after signing a waiver form. Recognized
users may include athletes, students, guests, staff, faculty with permission,
former athletes, family members, and visiting teams.
Equipment leaving the weight room shall be checked out by the supervisor
and recorded at the supervisor’s desk.
The on-duty supervisors have authority over all weight room conduct and
use of equipment and may expel an athlete from the facility for failure to
follow instructions.
