  25   DOUBLE-ARM LINEAR BOUND
Intensity level: Medium
Direction: Horizontal and vertical
Starting position: Get into a comfortable, upright stance with feet
hip-width apart.
Arm action: Double arm
Preparatory movement: Jog at a comfortable pace; begin the drill
with the right foot forward.
Upward movement: Push off with the right foot as it contacts the
ground. During push-off, bring the left leg forward by flexing the
thigh to a position approximately parallel with the ground, the knee
at 90 degrees, and the ankle dorsiflexed. During this flight phase of
the drill, reach forward with both arms. Sequence the arm swing so
that they come forward from behind the body into landing, and
forward from the hips to reach in front of the body during takeoff.
Downward movement: Land with an active flat-foot on the left leg
and immediately repeat the sequence on the opposite side.
Note: A bound is an exaggeration of the running gait; the goal is to
cover as great a distance as possible during each stride.
COMMON ERRORS
Not achieving sufficient horizontal velocity to initiate the
driving action of the bound
Coordination errors between arm and leg action; both
arms should be forward as the athlete takes off and
behind as the athlete lands
Overreaching with the lead leg
