Vitamins
Vitamins are organic substances (i.e., containing carbon atoms)
needed in very small amounts to perform specific metabolic
functions (71, 166). Vitamins typically act as coenzymes, facilitating
numerous reactions in the body. For instance, B vitamins help the
body make energy from the metabolism of carbohydrates. Table
10.13 describes the functions and some food sources of individual
vitamins.
