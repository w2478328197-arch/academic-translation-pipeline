Developing a standard of care is a collaborative responsibility. In
the law of negligence, standard of care is the degree of care that a
reasonable person should exercise (17). Standard of care for the
strength and conditioning professional refers to the degree of
caution and prudence that a reasonable person would exercise in
similar situations. The strength and conditioning professional is
expected to act according to their education, training, and
certification. Therefore, developing a mission statement and a list of
program objectives should involve not only the strength and
conditioning department but also the institution, including, but not
limited to, the athletic administration and the sports medicine
department. The more people participating, the better, so that all
involved groups and individuals take ownership and commit to
achieving the strength and conditioning program’s mission, goals,
and objectives.
