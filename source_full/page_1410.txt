depicts sprinting as a type of human locomotion in which the
displacement of a body mass is the aftereffect from energy produced
and is delivered through the collective coiling and extension of
spring-like actions within muscle architecture (11, 29, 37, 39).
During a complete running cycle, one spring compresses and propels
the sprinter’s body forward. Simultaneously, the other spring swings
forward in preparation for ground contact.
