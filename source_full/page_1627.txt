Comments
Each mesocycle is considered a block of training and contains two or three individual microcycles;
unloading mesocycles are strategically placed to occur before the next mesocycle during the off-season
and preseason and are at key microcycles during the in-season.
This program incorporates variations in volume-load, training intensity, and targeted training exercises.
Repetition maximum loads are not used, as training to failure has been shown to reduce power output
and increase the risk of overtraining. Therefore, percentage ranges based off of RM loads are used to
deal with the accumulated fatigue associated with in-season training and competitions.
It is important to note that these are the target sets and that 3 to 5 warm-up sets should be performed
to provide an appropriate specific warm-up for the targeted loadings.
Refer to table 18.8 (page 544) for the relationship between the %RM and the number of repetitions
allowed and table 18.9 (page 547) for the estimation of a 1RM.
Refer to table 18.15 (page 561) for rest period length assignment based on repetition goal.
