Catastrophe Theory
According to Hardy (24), assessment of the cognitive and somatic
dimensions of arousal can sharpen the ability to predict (and
therefore control) their impact on performance. Earlier assumptions
associated with the inverted-U theory held that increases in arousal
beyond the optimal level resulted in gradual, proportionate declines
in performance. However, common observation tells us that this is
not always the case—an athlete may suffer a severe and
