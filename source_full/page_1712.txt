Psychological Measures
Up to this point, we have discussed exclusively quantitative
measurements of performance, training, or physiology. However, it
has been suggested that questionnaires using subjective measures
may be more useful for monitoring athletes and their risk of
overtraining (115). To that end, numerous survey instruments have
been developed to help monitor athlete perceptions of training
status and recovery. Table 24.3 lists a number of these tools that
strength and conditioning professionals may find helpful to use.
