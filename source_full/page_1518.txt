Exercise Economy
A measure of the energy cost of activity at a given exercise velocity
is referred to as exercise economy. Athletes with a high exercise
economy expend less energy during exercise to maintain a given
exercise velocity (e.g., running speed). Several investigators have
suggested that exercise economy is an important factor in successful
performance in running events (71), with better performers also
having a slightly shorter stride length and greater stride frequency
compared to less successful performers (106). During cycling,
exercise economy can be affected by body mass size, cycling
velocity, and aerodynamic positioning (7, 41, 42). For cyclists, an
increase in body mass and cycling velocity as well as an inefficient
body position generate greater wind resistance, resulting in a
decrease in exercise economy. It has been demonstrated that elite
swimmers are much more economical than non-elite swimmers (6)
and use less oxygen at any given swimming velocity. The most
profound impact on exercise economy during swimming can be
observed when swimming technique becomes more efficient. As
improvements in stroke mechanics occur, energy demand for a given
swimming velocity is reduced (146). Training to improve exercise
economy is critical for aerobic endurance athletes.
