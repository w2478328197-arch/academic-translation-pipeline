Preparatory movement: Jog at a comfortable pace; begin the drill
with the left foot forward.
Upward movement: Push off with the left foot as it contacts the
ground. During push-off, bring the right leg forward by flexing the
thigh to a position approximately parallel with the ground and the
knee at 90 degrees, with the ankle stiff and dorsiflexed.
During this flight phase of the drill, reach forward with the left arm.
Downward movement: Attack the ground with an active flat-foot
contact. Land on the right leg, and immediately repeat the sequence
on the opposite side.
Note: A linear bound is an exaggeration of the running gait; the goal
is to cover as great a distance as possible during each stride.
COMMON ERRORS
Not achieving sufficient horizontal velocity to initiate the
driving action of the bound
Not achieving sufficient height with the lead leg in the
flight phase to attack the ground with enough force to
accelerate the athlete forward
Overreaching with the lead leg and then not driving
down under the hips
