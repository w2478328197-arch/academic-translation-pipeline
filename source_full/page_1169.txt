  19   SINGLE-ARM LANDMINE ROW
Setup and Starting Position
While standing facing away from the axis of rotation of the
landmine barbell, assume a hip-width stance with the unfixed
side of the barbell just outside of the working-side leg with the
toes behind the barbell sleeve.
While maintaining a 25- to 45-degree knee angle, hinge at the
hips until able to grasp the end of the barbell with one hand
while the elbow is extended and reaching downward. In this
position the forward trunk inclination should be roughly 30 to
60 degrees relative to the ground. This trunk angle should
remain constant throughout the movement (80).
Upward Movement Phase
The upward movement phase (i.e., concentric phase) is initiated
from the starting position and ends at the top position where
the barbell reaches the greatest angle relative to the floor and
contacts the torso.

retracting the shoulder girdle, extending the glenohumeral joint,
and flexing the elbow to pull the barbell along the natural arc of
the landmine barbell device (80).
Downward Movement Phase
Allow the shoulder girdle to protract, the glenohumeral joint to
flex, and the elbow to extend simultaneously to return to the
starting position (80).
Note: As part of a balanced training program, this movement is
performed on both sides of the body.
