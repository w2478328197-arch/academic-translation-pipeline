Power Exercises
   40   PUSH PRESS
This exercise consists of quickly and forcefully pushing the bar from
the shoulders to over the head. Although the ascent consists of two
phases, the upward movement of the bar occurs in one continuous
motion without interruption. Both the push press and the push jerk
exercises involve a rapid hip and knee extension that accelerates the
bar off the shoulders, followed immediately by movements that
position the bar overhead. The technique used to attain this final bar
position varies, however. In the push press, the hip and knee
extension thrust is only forceful enough to drive the bar one-half to
two-thirds the distance overhead. From this height, the bar is
pressed out to the overhead position, with the hips and knees
remaining fully extended after the thrust.
