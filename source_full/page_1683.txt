In an effort to maximize the training effect, it may be necessary
to include blocks of training that highly tax the individual in an
attempt to attain the greatest training response possible. Examples
of this can include the high-volume general preparation phases
found in many strength and conditioning programs (see chapter 18
for more details). When implemented correctly, the resulting longer-
term training effect can be quite substantial. But a problem arises
when these phases of training have too high a volume or intensity
and are beyond the recovery capacity of the individual or team,
which can give way to overtraining. But what exactly is overtraining?
