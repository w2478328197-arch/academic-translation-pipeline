Anaerobic Capacity
  14   300-YARD (274 M) SHUTTLE RUN
Equipment
A stopwatch with at least 0.1-second resolution
Two parallel lines 25 yards (23 m) apart on a flat surface (figure 14.14)
Personnel
One timer, two line judges
Procedure
1. Pair off athletes of similar ability.
2. Position two athletes immediately behind one line, facing the other line.
3. On an auditory signal, the athletes sprint to the line 25 yards (23 m) away, making foot
contact with it, then immediately sprint back to the first line. Six such round trips are made as
fast as possible without stopping (6 × 50 yd = 300 yd [274 m]).
4. On completion of the first trial, record both athletes’ times to the nearest 0.1 second and start
a clock to time a 5-minute rest interval. As each pair of athletes completes the first trial, they
may walk and stretch but must stay alert for the starting time on the second trial.
5. After the rest period, the pair of athletes does another trial.
6. The average of two trials is recorded to the nearest 0.1 second.
Note: Descriptive data for the 300-yard (274 m) shuttle are presented in table 14.14 near the end
