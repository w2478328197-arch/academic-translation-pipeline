The endocrine system interacts with the nervous, immune, and
musculoskeletal systems to create a network vital for health and
athletic performance. Hormones regulate cellular activities, including
muscle synthesis, recovery, and overall physiological well-being, by
balancing 
anabolic 
and 
catabolic 
processes. 
Strength 
and
conditioning professionals must understand the endocrine system to
design training programs that enhance performance, recovery, and
injury prevention. This knowledge allows for manipulating hormonal
responses to exercise, supporting adaptations, and preventing
overtraining. The system’s interplay has far-reaching effects on
athletic performance and broader health concerns such as metabolic
syndrome, insulin resistance, and cardiovascular health.
