Resistance Training Machine Area
Each station is easily accessed (a minimum of 30 in. [76 cm] between
machines; 36 in. [91 cm] is optimal).
Area is free of loose bolts, screws, cables, and chains.
Proper selectorized pins are used and there is adequate storage or hanging
space for equipment accessories.
Securing straps and locking mechanisms must be functional.
Functional parts are properly lubricated.
Protective padding is free of cracks and tears.
No protruding screws or parts need tightening or removed.
Belts, chains, and cables are aligned with machine parts.
No worn parts (e.g., frayed cable, loose chains, worn bolts, cracked joints)
exist.
Resistance Training Free Weight Area
Each bench or area is easily accessible (a minimum of 30 in. [76 cm] access
clearance; 36 in. [91 cm] is optimal).
Olympic bars are properly spaced (a minimum of 36 inches [91 cm])
between ends.
Dumbbell racks and other equipment are placed at least 6 inches (15 cm)
away from mirrors.
All equipment is returned after use to avoid obstruction of pathway.
Safety equipment (e.g., belts, collars, safety bars) is used and returned.
Protective padding is free of cracks and tears.
Bolts and apparatus parts (e.g., collars and attachments) are secure and
tightly fastened.
Olympic bars turn properly and are properly lubricated and tightened.
Benches, weight racks, standards, and similar equipment are secured to the
floor or wall.
Nonfunctional or broken equipment is removed from the area or labeled with
an “out of service” sign.
Weightlifting Area
Olympic bars are properly spaced (a minimum of 36 in. [91 cm]) between
ends.
All equipment is returned after use to avoid obstruction of lifting area.
Olympic bars turn properly and are properly lubricated and tightened.
