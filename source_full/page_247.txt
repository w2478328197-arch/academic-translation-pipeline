Amine Hormone Interactions
Amine hormones are indeed synthesized from amino acids, including
those derived from tyrosine (such as epinephrine, norepinephrine,
and dopamine) and tryptophan (such as serotonin), and primarily
exert their effects by binding to membrane receptors. These
hormones typically act through secondary messenger systems,
similar to polypeptide hormones. For example, epinephrine and
norepinephrine interact with G protein-coupled receptors to initiate
signaling cascades involving cAMP or other secondary messengers.
