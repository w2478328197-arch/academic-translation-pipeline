Overtraining Continuum
All the different types and severities of overtraining and overreaching
can collectively make an overtraining continuum (figure 24.7).
Although some overlap may exist between each of the categories,
this continuum illustrates how each successive level becomes more
serious for the athlete and requires longer and more intense
recovery.

It should be apparent now that not all forms of overtraining are
identical (28, 126, 129), and the resulting OTS is not a simple
problem with simple solutions. Indeed, it has been repeatedly
reported that many factors contribute to this problem, and whether
it is due to training for aerobic endurance sports (28, 46) or
resistance exercise (37, 43), it is extremely complex (46, 49, 84, 88,
89, 139) and multidisciplinary (75, 107). The net result is that there
are many risk factors for overtraining (110, 139) that must be
recognized to avoid this problem. As evidenced by some who believe
there is no evidence of fatigue accumulation from resistance exercise
