assessments:
Category I. Daily tests, easy to administer, inexpensive, rapid
turnaround of results, convenient for the coach and athlete
Category II. Every few days, more complex, longer
questionnaires, slightly more inconvenient to the athlete than
category I, rapid turnaround of results
Category III. Occasionally administered (e.g., once a month),
more involved and intrusive, more expensive, longer
turnaround time for the results
Figure 24.16  Overtraining risk factors,
considerations, and strategies to minimize risk of
developing an overtraining syndrome.
Overtraining Risk Factors
Lack of close monitoring of the training program (rest days, prior exposure
to stressful training)
Training and activities outside the weight room, which can be a problem
Use of virtual coaching with no face-to-face contact
Lack of any objective monitoring
Training higher level and more fit athletes
Unmonitored recovery training sessions
Training during Olympic or world championship training blocks (i.e., final
training phase for an ultimate competition)
Failure to implement an appropriate training taper
Coaching performed by someone with little coaching experience or training
Change of training environment (e.g., heat or cold, humidity, altitude)
