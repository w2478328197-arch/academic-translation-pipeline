programs (inclusive of resistance exercise) rather than
short-term programs to enhance physical fitness and
reduce the risk of injury.
Early sport specialization is defined as children engaging in
intensive, year-round (>8 months) training and/or competition in a
single sport, typically beginning during the prepubertal years (131).
Traditional early specialization sports included gymnastics, dance,
and swimming; however, there is a growing trajectory of youth
opting to specialize in other sports at younger ages (21). In many
cases, early sport specialization results in young athletes
accumulating weekly training volumes that exceed recommended
guidelines (21, 202). Even though some coaches argue that early
sport specialization is the key to success, this notion has been
challenged in the literature (21, 114, 131). Notably, meta-analytical
data from 51 studies indicated that while sampling a diverse range
of sports led to more measured initial progress, it was associated
with more sustainable long-term development toward sporting
excellence (96). Research has indicated that youth who participate in
a single sport or specialize in a single position at an early age may
be at a greater risk of serious injury (114, 150, 238) and overuse
