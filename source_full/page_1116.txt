The application of maximal and supramaximal AEL are best suited
for multijoint resistance training exercises, such as the bench press,
back squat, and front squat with the use of either weight releasers
or computer-driven devices (figure 17.7) (92, 98, 136). For example,
Taber and colleagues (136) report significant improvements in
concentric velocity with loads between 30% and 80% of the 1RM
during the bench press when a maximal AEL (100% of the 1RM) is
implemented. Conversely, when supramaximal AEL is applied with
loads between 105% and 120% of the concentric 1RM, slower
concentric movement velocities occur during the repetition when the
concentric phase is performed with loads between 30% and 80% of
the 1RM (25, 78, 91-93, 142). However, it is plausible that the
differential between the AEL and the load lifted during the concentric
phase of the movement mitigates the acute performance gains. For
example, if a differential >40% between the AEL and the concentric
load is programmed, significant acute increases in velocity and
power occur during the concentric phase of the repetition (92).
