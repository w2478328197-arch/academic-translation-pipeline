Arm action: None
Preparatory movement: Step from the box.
Downward movement: Land flat footed on the ground with both
feet, quickly absorbing the impact on landing. Ensure the hips,
knees, and toes align, with the trunk vertical. Step back onto the box
and repeat.
Note: This drill teaches joint alignment and landing mechanics and is
often the first drill used in plyometric teaching and rehabilitation
progressions. Also, this can also be performed as a single-leg landing
drill.
COMMON ERRORS
Absorbing ground reaction forces while landing through
hip and knee flexion, rather than landing and
instantaneously holding position to develop load
tolerance
Losing the knee alignment with the toes when landing in
a knee valgus position, potentially compromising the
knee joint
