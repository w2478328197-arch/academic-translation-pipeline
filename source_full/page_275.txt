Specifically, T3 enhances protein synthesis by increasing the

Additionally, T4’s conversion to T3 in peripheral tissues ensures a
steady supply of active hormones to meet ongoing metabolic needs.
Thyroid hormones also help regulate lipolysis and glucose
metabolism, which are essential for providing the necessary energy
substrates during and after resistance exercise. By modulating these
processes, thyroid hormones help maintain energy balance and
facilitate recovery. Furthermore, thyroid hormones interact with
other endocrine responses during resistance training. For example,
they can enhance the action of GH and IGF-1, both of which are
vital for muscle growth and repair. This synergistic effect amplifies
the overall anabolic environment, promoting more effective muscle
adaptation and strength gains. Thus, thyroid hormones are integral
to immediate and long-term resistance exercise adaptations. They
ensure metabolic homeostasis, support energy production, enhance
protein synthesis, and interact with other hormones to facilitate
muscle growth and repair. Understanding their role highlights the
