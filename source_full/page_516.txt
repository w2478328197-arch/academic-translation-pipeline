Psychological Factors That Can Affect an
Athlete After Injury
Psychological concerns emerge throughout the process of injury
recovery and often include timelines of rehabilitation to return to
training and competition. Athletes must process a variety of conflicts
once an injury has been sustained, including attending to and
listening to the body, tending to the pain versus pushing through the
pain, and interpersonal challenges and conflicts with teammates.
Also, injuries can be a challenge to an athlete’s identity and concerns
about time left in the season or his or her career and falling behind
teammates (60). Proper management of their relative effort and
intensity to avoid future reinjury becomes a primary goal. Pushing
through pain and injury may be falsely viewed as heroic and
expected by teammates, parents, coaches, and fans (63).
The Cognitive Appraisal Model (66) proposes four key components
that may influence how athletes judge their injury and how it affects
their emotional and recovery response due to injury (figure 9.5). By
understanding this model, strength and conditioning professionals
can offer more support and guidance to help athletes return to their
preexisting confidence and performance levels.
