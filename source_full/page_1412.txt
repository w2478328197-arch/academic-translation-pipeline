maximal-velocity phase of sprinting (0.09-0.11 s) (104, 105). In the
context of COD, as the intended angle of direction change (i.e.,
deflection of center of mass) increases, the ground contact time of
the main execution plant foot typically increases, attributed to the
greater braking force, negative work (i.e., knee flexion), and
deceleration demands associated when having to reduce momentum
with sharper directional changes (36). As a result, this prolongs the
contact time to increase braking impulse to reduce momentum along
the original direction followed by propulsive impulse to enable
acceleration toward the new intended direction of travel. In view of
this, COD actions <45 degrees generally can be considered fast SSC
activities, whereas direction changes >45 degrees generally can be
considered slower SSC activities, though this depends on entry
velocity and an athlete’s physical capacity (36).
