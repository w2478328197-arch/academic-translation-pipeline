Period
Duration
Description
Multiyear training
plans
2-4 years
This contains interlinked annual training plans; the most common
multiyear plan is the quadrennial plan.
Annual training
plan
1 year
The overall annual training plan can contain a single or multiple
macrocycles. It can be subdivided into preparatory, competitive, and
transitional periods of training.
Macrocycle
Several months
to a year
It contains preparatory, competitive, and transitional periods of
training. If it encompasses one year, some authors refer to this as an
annual plan.
Mesocycle
2-6 weeks
This medium-sized training cycle is sometimes called a block of
training. It consists of microcycles that are linked together. The most
common duration is 4 weeks.
Microcycle
Several days to 2
weeks
This small-sized training cycle is comprised of multiple workouts. It
can range from several days to 2 weeks in duration; the most
common duration is 1 week (7 days).
Training day
1 day
One training day can contain multiple sessions. It is designed in the
context of the microcycle in which it is contained.
Training session
Several hours
It generally consists of several hours of training. If the session has
>30 min of rest between bouts of training, it should be considered as
having multiple workouts.
