Through an understanding of how energy transfer occurs, more
efficient and productive training programs can be designed. After
defining essential bioenergetics terminology and explaining the role
of adenosine triphosphate (ATP), this chapter discusses the three
basic energy systems that work to replenish ATP in human skeletal
muscle. Then we look at substrate depletion and repletion, especially
as they relate to fatigue and recovery; bioenergetic factors that limit
performance; and aerobic and anaerobic contributions to oxygen
uptake. Finally, the metabolic specificity of training is discussed.
