Rating of Perceived Exertion and Repetitions in
Reserve
Beyond percentages of 1RM, other methods of load prescription are available to
strength and conditioning professionals. The rating of perceived exertion
(RPE) provides a subjective measurement of work based on the perception of
an individual. While the original RPE scale (i.e., 6-20) was based on aerobic
activity (15), a modified CR10 scale (i.e., 0-10) has been examined by
researchers when monitoring resistance training exercise intensity within an
individual set (92, 135) and during entire training sessions (i.e., session RPE)
(37, 107, 181). Similarly, repetitions in reserve (RIR) provides a subjective
measure of the perceived number of repetitions that theoretically could be
