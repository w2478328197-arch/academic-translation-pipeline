CHAPTER 6
ADAPTATIONS TO AEROBIC
TRAINING
Kate Baldwin, PhD, and Glen B. Deakin, PhD
The authors would like to acknowledge the significant contributions of Ann Swank and
Carwyn P.M. Sharp to this chapter.
After completing this chapter, you will be able to
identify and describe the impact of chronic aerobic endurance
training on the physiological characteristics of the
cardiovascular, respiratory, nervous, muscular, bone and
connective tissue, and endocrine systems;
recognize the interaction between aerobic endurance training
and optimizing physiological responses of all body systems;
identify and describe external factors that influence adaptations
to chronic aerobic exercise including altitude, heat, smoking
and vaping, blood doping, and detraining; and
recognize the physiological differences between altitude
training and heat acclimation.
