Formula (on inlaid rubber flooring): DEPTH: a
suggested user space of 8-10 ft (2.4-3.0 m) for a
weightlifting exercise multiplied by WIDTH: actual bar
length (6-7 ft [1.8-2.1 m]) + a double-wide safety cushion
of 6 ft (1.8 m)
Formula (on an elevated wood platform): DEPTH:
lifting platform length (typically 8 ft [2.4 m]) + a safety
cushion of 3 ft (0.9 m) multiplied by WIDTH: lifting
platform width (typically 8 ft [2.4 m]) + a double-wide
safety cushion of 6 ft (1.8 m)
Example 1 (on inlaid rubber flooring): DEPTH: (10 ft
[user space]) × WIDTH: (7 ft [bar] + 6 ft [double-wide
safety cushion]) = 130 ft2

approximations):
DEPTH: (2.5 m [platform length] + 1 m [safety cushion])

safety cushion]) ≈ 16 m2

Formula: The minimum space need is based on the area
(A) of a circle (A = π × r2): The radius (r) of the circle is
comprised of the actual bar length (5-7 ft [1.5-2.1 m]) + a
suggested user space of 2 ft (0.6 m) for a landmine
exercise + a safety cushion of 3 ft (0.9 m). A minimum of
90 degrees of movement is required for the range of
motion at the user end of the bar for a landmine exercise;
90 degrees is one-quarter of a 360-degree circle. Thus, the
formula is (π × [actual bar length + user space + safety
cushion]2) divided by 4. (Use 3.14 for the value of π.)
Example 1: If using a 7 ft Olympic bar for the landmine
squat exercise: (π × [7 ft {bar} + 2 ft {user space} + 3 ft
{safety cushion}]2) ÷ 4 ≈ 113 ft2
