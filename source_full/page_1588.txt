levels: periodization, planning, and programming (table 22.1).

When beginning the construction of a periodized training plan the first level, or
periodization level, provides the scaffold from which the training process is
developed (33, 35, 38). At this level, based on the competitive schedule, the
annual training plan is subdivided into various periods and phases to provide a
basic framework from which the detailed training plan is established. At the
second level, or planning level, a training model (i.e., parallel, sequential, or
emphasis model) is selected to guide programmatic decisions. Finally, once the
models of training are selected and aligned with the various periods and phases
contained within the annual plan, they can be used to inform the third level of the
process, which involves the actual construction of the training program (41). The
programming level is where the various modes, methods, and targeted training
strategies are implemented and the actual training loads (i.e., intensities and
volumes) and training structures (e.g., loading patterns and set types) are
prescribed (35, 41).
