Adverse Effects
The duration, dosage, and timing of HMB supplementation have
notably varied in the scientific literature. In nearly every published
investigation related to HMB supplementation, 3 to 6 grams per day
was ingested. Three grams per day (often divided into several
doses) is the most common dosage used in published studies. The
majority of studies investigating HMB have used the calcium salt of
HMB—HMB-Ca. Another form of HMB, HMB-free acid, has been
investigated as well (192). The safety of HMB supplementation has
been widely studied, and currently, the consensus is that HMB
presents no known adverse effects.

Nutritional Muscle Buffers
During high-intensity anaerobic exercise, a significant accumulation
of hydrogen ions (H+) is coupled with a reduction of pH within
skeletal muscle and has been shown to adversely affect performance
(182). The ability to regulate H+ concentration in skeletal muscle
during high-intensity exercise has been termed muscle buffering
capacity (MBC). There is a strong positive relationship between
MBC and exercise performance (repeated-sprint ability, high-intensity
exercise capacity, anaerobic threshold, and training volume) (62). In
fact, researchers have demonstrated a positive relationship between
exercise performance and MBC in athletes who participate in sports
such as basketball, soccer, hockey, cycling, crew (rowing), triathlons,
and sprinting (62). In theory, improving MBC by training or
