Plyometrics and Other Forms of
Exercise
Plyometric exercise is only one part of an athlete’s overall training
program. Many sports and activities use multiple energy systems or
require other forms of exercise to properly prepare athletes for
competition. Each energy system and sport-specific need must be
included in a well-designed training program.
Plyometric Exercise and Resistance
Training
A combination of plyometrics and resistance training during a
training cycle should be structured to allow maximal efficiency and
physical improvement. The following list and table 19.8 provide
guidelines for developing a combined program:
Combine lower body resistance training with upper body
plyometrics, and upper body resistance training with lower
body plyometrics.
Performing heavy resistance training sessions and plyometric
sessions on the same day is not usually recommended (17,
24). However, many athletes may benefit from complex
training, a combination of resistance training and plyometric
training within the same training session to enhance fast and
slow force expression.
If athletes perform this type of training, adequate recovery is
needed between plyometrics and other high-intensity training.
Plyometrics can comprise a stand-alone training session or be
incorporated into a resistance training session. Incorporating
plyometrics into a resistance training session may have
neuromuscular 
recruitment 
benefits 
through 
facilitating
postactivation potentiation during biomechanically similar
movements (22).
