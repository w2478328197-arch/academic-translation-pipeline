and the feet hip-width apart and flat on the floor.
2. Pushing the feet into the floor and squeezing the glutes, lift the

MUSCLES AFFECTED
gluteus maximus, hamstrings, erector spinae
COMMON ERROR
Using an excessive range of motion and hyperextending
the lower back
  26   INCHWORM
1. Stand erect with the feet placed shoulder-width apart.
2. While slightly flexing the knees, flex forward at the waist and
place hands shoulder-width apart flat on the floor.
3. The weight of the body should be shifted back (i.e., not directly
over the hands), with the buttocks high in the air—imagine
making an inverted V with the body.
4. Move the hands alternately forward, as if taking short steps
with the hands, until the body is in a push-up position.
