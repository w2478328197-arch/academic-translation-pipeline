After consideration of the predispositions related to the masters
athlete’s physical condition, a plyometric program should be
designed according to the same guidelines as outlined for adult
athletes, with the following emphases:
Intensity and volume should be carefully considered relative to
the performance capacities of the individual.
Cautions regarding decreased strength capacities, potential for
degenerative 
joint

and 
decreased

capacities associated with aging should be applied.
It is important to note how the masters athlete feels after
training; soreness may occur, but the program should be
modified if chronic or excessive pain or discomfort is present.
