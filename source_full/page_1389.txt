  52   LATERAL TRAVEL PUSH-UP
Intensity level: High
Direction: Lateral
Equipment: Medicine ball or a stable implement of similar height
Starting position: Get into a push-up position, with one hand on the
medicine ball (elbow partially flexed) and one hand on the ground,
elbow extended (not shown).
Preparatory movement: None
Downward movement: Keeping both hands in place, rapidly drop
down into a push-up deep enough so that the chest is level with the
top of the medicine ball (or lower).
Drill movement: Immediately and explosively push up and sideways
by fully extending the elbows. The trunk should move laterally
across the medicine ball while quickly switching hands (the hand
that was on the ground now lands on the medicine ball, and vice
versa). Immediately on landing, repeat the movement in the
opposite direction (alternate hands with each repetition).
Note: When the upper body is at maximal height during the upward
movement, both hands should be above the medicine ball.
COMMON ERRORS
Leading the vertical movement with the hips rather than
pushing through the upper body
Not achieving sufficient vertical height to travel across
the ball (which should not move)
