Coach influence. Does the sport coach actively support the
athlete during the rehab process? Is there pressure to return
as soon as possible with the athlete fearing losing his or her
position? Coaches should be attentive to the possibility that
athletes could be struggling with challenges related to conflicts
with coaches who may not understand the extent of their
injuries; suggest that they are weak; and may explicitly
minimize, invalidate, or dismiss the athlete’s struggles (60).
Behavioral Responses
Consider how the athlete behaves when facing the impact of injury
on his or her health and performance:
Adherence to rehabilitation. Does the athlete demonstrate
consistency and commitment to the active process?
Use of psychological skills. Are techniques such as goal
setting, imagery, and visualization integrated to improve their
recovery timeline?
Effort and intensity. How does the athlete’s level of motivation
change over time?
Malingering. Does the athlete exacerbate pain or readiness
levels to avoid future responsibilities or changes in their
timeline?
Risk-taking. Does the athlete engage in activities that have not
yet been cleared for participation? Do they avoid certain
activities because they feel they are not yet ready to perform?
