Intensity level: Medium to high
Direction: Horizontal and vertical
Equipment: Two to 10 barriers such as cones or hurdles
Starting position: Facing the first barrier, get into a comfortable,
single-leg upright stance with the contralateral hip and knee flexed
to 90 degrees.
Arm action: Double arm
Preparatory movement: Begin with a countermovement on the
hopping leg. Bring the other leg behind the body.
Upward movement: Explosively hop forward, driving from the hip,
knee, and ankle over the first barrier as tall as possible, using
necessary hip and knee flexion to clear the barrier.
Downward movement: Dorsiflex the ankles in preparation for an
active flat-foot landing in the starting position, and immediately
repeat the jump over the second barrier.
Note: Intensity level of this exercise can be increased from medium
to high by progressively increasing the height of the barrier (e.g.,
from a cone to a hurdle). Increasing the intensity of the hop may
increase the ground contact time and the amortization phase.
COMMON ERRORS
Incorrect hurdle height or a less-than-maximal push
from the ground to achieve full extension position when
clearing the hurdle
Inappropriate landing mechanics, which increases
ground contact time between hurdles
