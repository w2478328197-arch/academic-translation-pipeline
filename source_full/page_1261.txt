defined, plyometric exercise is a quick, powerful movement using a
prestretch, or countermovement, that involves the stretch-
shortening cycle (SSC) (64). Furthermore, plyometric exercises are
typically classified as being either ballistic or rebounding. Ballistic
exercises are those that minimally include contributions from the
neuromuscular component of the SSC (e.g., squat jump, medicine
ball overhead throw). In contrast, rebound exercises are those
that require physiomechanical SSC contributions (e.g., drop jumps or
repeat countermovement jumps). This classification is important
because it determines the specific neuromuscular and mechanical
demands of each exercise (37). The purpose of plyometric exercise
is to increase the power of subsequent movements by using both
the natural elastic components of muscle and tendon and the stretch
reflex. To effectively use plyometrics as part of a training program, it
is important to understand the mechanics and physiology of
plyometric exercise, the principles of plyometric training program
design, and the methods of safely and effectively performing specific
plyometric exercises.
