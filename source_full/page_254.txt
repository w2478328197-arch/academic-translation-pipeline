Influence of Sex Hormones
Testosterone, while primarily recognized as a male sex hormone,
also plays a significant role in women, who naturally have only about
5% to 10% of the circulating testosterone levels found in men due
to the absence of testes. Research on testosterone responses in
women following resistance exercise has yielded mixed results. Most
studies have not shown a notable acute increase in total
testosterone after workouts; if increases are observed, they are
generally small (15, 23, 51, 56, 81, 134). However, some studies
note these increases specifically in free testosterone, which is not
bound to binding proteins and thus can interact freely with cells
(133).
A study found that younger women experienced a modest but
significant increase in serum testosterone after performing six sets
of squats at a 10RM with 2 minutes of rest between sets at 80% of
the 1 RM (111). Additionally, Vingren and colleagues observed that
highly resistance-trained men and women exhibited acute rises in
free testosterone following heavy resistance exercises. However, the
values in women were significantly lower than those in men (133).
