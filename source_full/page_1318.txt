  12   JUMP OVER BARRIER
Intensity level: Medium
Direction: Horizontal and vertical
Equipment: A barrier such as a cone or hurdle
Starting position: Get into a comfortable, upright stance with feet
hip-width apart.
Arm action: Double arm
Preparatory movement: Begin with a countermovement.
Upward movement: Take off with a full triple extension of hips,
knees, and ankles. (Note: The second photo should show the athlete
in a tall position to demonstrate full triple extension after takeoff. It
also does not yet show the feet in the correct position for landing.)
Jump over the barrier with both legs, maximizing the height of the
center of mass and using only sufficient hip and knee flexion to clear
the barrier. Keep the knees and feet together without lateral
deviation.
Downward movement: Dorsiflex the ankles in preparation for a
stable flat-foot landing. Land in the starting position and reset.
Repeat until the set is complete.
