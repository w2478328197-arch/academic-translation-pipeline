   15  90-SECOND BOX JUMP TEST
Equipment
Stopwatch
A box with the dimensions of 18 inches (46 cm) in height and 30 inches (76 cm) in width
A nonslip surface on which to perform the test in which the athlete can safely land and jump
Personnel
One recorder/tester
Procedure
1. The purpose of this test is to observe how many times the athlete can jump over the box in a
left to right fashion over a duration of 90 seconds. The athlete begins on top of the box and
then jumps down laterally to one side before jumping back up to the top of the box again. The
athlete then repeats this procedure in the opposite direction. Each time the athlete reaches the
top of the box counts as 1 repetition.
2. The athlete must leave the ground with two feet and touch the top of the box with two feet
before jumping down to the other side. The athlete is not permitted to step up onto the box.
3. This is repeated for as many times as the athlete is able to during the 90-second duration of
the test. The tester should record the number of hops taken with proper form over the course
of the test.
Note: Descriptive data for the 90-second box jump test are presented in table 14.15 near the end
of the chapter.

  16   PARTIAL CURL-UP
The partial curl-up test measures the muscular endurance of the abdominal muscles. It is favored
over the sit-up test because it eliminates the use of the hip flexor muscles.
