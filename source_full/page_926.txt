  30   LUNGE WALK WITH FRONT LEG
EXTENSION
1. Take an exaggerated step directly forward with the right leg and
assume a lunge stance, keeping the right knee directly over the
right foot and the rear (left) knee about an inch or so off the
floor (note: this preparatory position is not shown).
2. Flex the torso forward with the hands reaching toward the right
foot. From this position, lift the hips up and back, extending the
right knee to a fully (or nearly so) extended position. This will
cause the left knee to extend as well and result in a stretch of
the hamstring muscles.
3. After the stretch is held, extend the torso to an erect position,
step the rear (left) foot forward, and repeat on the other side.
MUSCLES AFFECTED
hamstrings, gluteus maximus, iliopsoas, quadriceps
COMMON ERROR
Losing postural integrity during the movement
