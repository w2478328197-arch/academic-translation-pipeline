Assume a position under the yoke with the bar positioned on
top of the upper trapezius muscles, with the feet positioned
underneath the hips and the rest of the body symmetrically
positioned under the frame. This should look something similar
to the starting position of a partial barbell back squat, except
that the hands are positioned on the vertical bars around lower
chest height.
Liftoff and Walking Phase
After strongly bracing the spine, forcibly push the feet into the
ground while pushing the elbows and hands forward into the
vertical bars to better stabilize the yoke.
While continuing to brace the spine and push the elbows and
hands forward, adopt a walking movement pattern in which the
spine remains vertical; initially focus on performing a series of
short and relatively fast steps.
While accelerating, increase the length of steps and further
reduce ground contact times.
Ending Position
After completing the set distance, flex the hips, knees, and
ankles to lower the frame carefully to the ground.
Maintain a strong braced spinal position to minimize the risk of
impact injury.
MAJOR MUSCLES INVOLVED
gastrocnemius, soleus, gluteus maximus, rectus femoris,
gluteus medius, erector spinae, internal and external
obliques
