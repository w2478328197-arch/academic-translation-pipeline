Arm action: Initiate the movement with a rotational drive of the hips
that continues through the trunk, bringing the ball across the body
so that it gains momentum. Using both arms, complete the
rotational arc of the ball so that it is released and thrown against the
wall as it passes the front hip. Catch the rebound, and immediately
repeat the movement.
Note: This exercise can be made more of a hip-initiated movement
by shifting the weight onto the rear foot and abducting the hip
(frontal plane) to bring weight onto the rear foot; this variation may
benefit baseball pitchers seeking more leg-driven throwing action.
Golfers may find it beneficial to rotate the hip in the transverse plane
(not move in the frontal plane). Both variations enable the rear hip
to initiate the action and accelerate the trunk rotation.
COMMON ERRORS
Rotating from the lumbar spine and trunk rather than
shifting weight to facilitate the hip drive
Over-relying on the upper body to throw the ball
compared to using the lower body and hip rotation to
enable a speed multiplication effect through the trunk to
achieve greater impulse on the ball
