Benefits of Bodyweight Training
The following are some of the benefits of bodyweight training (56):
Is specific to each individual’s anthropometrics
Often includes closed-chain activities that engage multiple joints
Strengthens several muscle groups simultaneously
Develops relative strength
Improves body control
Is a versatile form of training that can be done anywhere
Is a low-cost training alternative
Suspension training is an alternative method for performing
bodyweight resistance training that allows for the modification of the
resistance applied during numerous upper and lower body exercise
options (90). Performing this type of training uses devices, referred
to as suspension trainers (e.g., TRX®), that are typically constructed
with sturdy and adjustable straps that have handles or foot straps
attached to one end. These devices are generally suspended from a
singular or multiple centralized attachment points above the user’s
head (e.g., wall, squat rack, doorjamb, ceiling) allowing the straps to
hang perpendicular to the floor (31, 113). Athletes can hang from
the device by holding the strap handles or placing their feet in the
foot straps to manipulate the placement of their center of mass,
which allows for the intensity of the exercise to be manipulated
(139).
