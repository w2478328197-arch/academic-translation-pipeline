Athlete Training Status
The training status of athletes has been suggested to be an
important variable to consider when undertaking concurrent training
(11). While the majority of findings indicate that concurrent training
can benefit aerobic endurance performance of athletes of different
levels across different aerobic endurance disciplines (10), the
responses may be different. For example, a systematic review and
meta-analysis found that following a concurrent training program,
trained or highly trained aerobic endurance athletes did not
demonstrate any impairments to improvements in V̇ O2max; however,
untrained individuals did (65). Consequently, for aerobic endurance
athletes who have minimal resistance training experience or for
lesser trained aerobic endurance athletes, it may be necessary to
allow greater recovery times between training sessions, or apply a
slower progression of training variables (e.g., intensity, frequency, or
duration) or exercises compared to higher trained athletes.
