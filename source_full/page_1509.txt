Progressions and Regressions
A range of different mechanical and perceptual-cognitive factors
can be manipulated within agility drills, which can create
variation, new learning environments, and problems to solve
while also influencing the task complexity and cognitive loading,
which are summarized in table 20.7 (page 666). For example,
the pitch dimensions could be expanded (figure a), which can
increase the entry and exit velocities and momentums with a
variation in end zones for the attacker to target. To increase
game representativeness, the addition of static mannequins or
defenders can further replicate the chaotic environments that
are often encountered in sports such as American football
(figure b). Finally, most multidirectional sports have multiple
attackers and defenders; thus, to increase specificity, 2v2 small-
sided games (and other configurations) also can be used to
work on attacking and defensive transitions, organization, and
communication (figure c).
