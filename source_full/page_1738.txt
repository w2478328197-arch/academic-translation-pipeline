Assessing Athletic Program Needs
One of the more important considerations in building a strength and
conditioning facility is the needs and requirements of the athletes
and 
athletic 
program. 
The 
number 
of 
athletes, 
coaching
philosophies, athlete ages and experience levels, schedules and
availability, and equipment factors can affect the design of the
facility (12). When it is time to evaluate such needs, the facility
designers should be able to answer these questions:
How many athletes will use the facility? This is an important
consideration because the facility size will be greatly
influenced by the number of athletes using the facility at the
same time (i.e., at peak usage) and the total number who use
it per day, week, and season. The number of athletes will also
affect the scheduling and flow of the facility if two or more
groups are training simultaneously. Based on regulations such
as fire codes or other occupational health and safety
regulations, the city planning departments will specify the
maximum room capacity. In addition to the number of athletes
training, it is also essential to consider staffing and
supervision.

number 
of 
athletes 
being 
trained
simultaneously will affect the number of strength and
conditioning professionals required to safely and effectively
monitor the training session (16).
