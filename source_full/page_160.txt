Certain products provide resistance to vertical jumping with elastic
bands as a means of developing jumping power. However, the elastic
bands provide little resistance early in the jump when the large
gluteal and quadriceps muscles are capable of exerting great force.
The bands provide the greatest resistance while the jumper is in the
air—serving mainly to pull the jumper back to the ground, rather
than resist the muscles, and so increasing the jumper’s landing
velocity, which may increase injury risk.
