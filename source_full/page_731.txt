Reasons for Testing
Testing helps athletes and coaches objectively assess athletic talent
and identify physical abilities and areas in need of improvement. In
addition, test scores can be used in goal setting by setting attainable
benchmarks for athletes to aim for. Baseline measurements can be
used to establish starting points against which achievable training
goals can be set, and testing at regular intervals can help track an
athlete’s progress in reaching those goals and provide insight into
fatigue, readiness, and injury risk. Using tests as a basis for goal
setting allows coaches to set specific goals for individual athletes
that, when taken together, help to accomplish group or team
objectives (see chapter 9 for more information about goal setting).
Assessment of Athletic Talent
It is important for a coach to determine whether an individual has
the physical potential to play a sport at the competitive level of the
team. That judgment is not difficult if the candidate has already
excelled at the sport elsewhere and is of adequate body size.
However, in many cases, candidates have not clearly demonstrated
their competitive abilities or may lack experience in the sport. The
coach then needs some way of determining whether the candidate
has the necessary basic physical abilities that, in combination with
technique training and practice, could produce a competitive player.
Field tests serve as tools for such assessment, and this application of
testing is referred to as talent identification.
