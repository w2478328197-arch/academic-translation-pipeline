CHAPTER 8
SEX-RELATED DIFFERENCES
AND THEIR IMPLICATIONS
FOR RESISTANCE TRAINING
N. Travis Triplett, PhD, and Nicole Dabbs, PhD
After completing this chapter, you will be able to
discuss sex-related differences in physical characteristics and
muscular function and the implications for resistance training in
females;
recognize common injuries and conditions in females;
discuss responses and adaptations to resistance training in
females; and
understand resistance training recommendations for females.
Strength and conditioning professionals need to understand sex-
related differences in physical characteristics, body composition, and
physiological responses to resistance exercise when designing and
evaluating 
resistance 
training 
programs 
for 
females. 
An
understanding of these differences and the areas of concern that are
unique to female athletes can help to optimize performance and
decrease the risk of sport-related injuries.
