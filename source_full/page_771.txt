Assessing movement patterns can reveal indicators of injury susceptibility in young athletes.
Well-established screening tools such as the functional movement screen (FMS), tuck jump
assessment (TJA), landing error scoring system, and drop jump assessments are commonly used
for this purpose (123). Additionally, tailored movement screening protocols have been devised
specific to particular sports, aiming to pinpoint injury risks, performance limitations, or even talent
identification opportunities. Tailored training programs can be developed by strength and
conditioning professionals to target specific individual needs, including addressing movement
limitations.
Multiple movement screens assess parts of the AMSC spectrum, but consensus has not been
reached regarding which are most appropriate for a given cohort. One narrative review (122)
included 10 movement screens, including but not limited to the FMS, TJA, resistance training skills
battery (RTSB), and back squat assessment (BSA). Interested readers are directed to the review by
Pullen and colleagues (122) for more detailed information on the methodologies, reliabilities, and
strengths and limitations of each of these tests when selecting the screening test that is the most
appropriate for the environment.
