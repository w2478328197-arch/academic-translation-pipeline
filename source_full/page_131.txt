done in dynamic movements such as jumping or sprinting.
Finally, power refers to how quickly work is performed. In its
simplest form, power is the amount of work divided by the time over
which the force is applied. Athletes in many sports aim to maximize
power output, meaning they strive to apply as much force as
possible over the shortest time frame to achieve the required
movement.
For all the equations in this chapter to work out correctly,
consistent units must be used. In the International System of Units
(SI, abbreviated from French), the worldwide standard, force is
measured in newtons (N), length (e.g., distance and displacement)
in meters (m), work in joules (J, i.e., newton-meters, or N · m), time
in seconds (s), and power in watts (W, i.e., J/s). The appropriate SI
units for the equations can be obtained from other common units
using the factors listed in table 2.1 (47a).
