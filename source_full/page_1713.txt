How long is the questionnaire, and how many items need to
be completed?
Has the questionnaire been validated?
Is the wording easy to understand for the intended audience?
Is it easy to process the data for a rapid turnaround?
Can the questionnaire be answered without any coercion?
Is the questionnaire sensitive to an overtraining stimulus?
All of these issues are essential to obtain helpful information. In
addition to overtraining-related surveys, several instruments are
available to determine the coaching motivational climate (44). It has
been clearly shown that the motivational orientation of the athlete as
well as the climate created by the coach has a large positive effect
on athlete effort, enjoyment, and retention in the sport. These
assessments do not need to be administered as often as training-
related questionnaires but can be a critical part of the athlete
monitoring program that is often overlooked. Needless to say, the
coaching climate is highly dependent on coaching strategies.
Another related aspect is the mental health and well-being of the
coach, which can directly affect athletes’ performance satisfaction
(7). Finally, it must be realized that the psychological well-being of
the athlete may be enhanced by reinforcing mindfulness traits (12,
20), creating both a caring climate and a task-oriented climate (44),
and being aware of mood states (95). It stands to reason that
mentally healthy athletes may be able to withstand the rigors of
stressful training better (64).
