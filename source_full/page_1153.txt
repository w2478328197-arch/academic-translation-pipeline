The sandbag can be positioned lengthways in the intended
direction of the carry or positioned across the body. Due to the
width of the sandbag, it may be necessary to rotate the hips
outward, with the elbows inside the knees.
Wedge the fingers under the sides of the sandbag so that the
hands act as hooks under each side of the sandbag.
Liftoff Position
After strongly bracing the spine and ensuring a strong grip on
the sandbag, forcibly push the feet into the ground and row the
sandbag into your chest.
If the sandbag is quite heavy, transition into a full squat
position. However, if the sandbag is light to moderately heavy, it
may be possible to bypass the full squat position and, via
forceful hip extension, stand up from this position and initiate
the carrying phase.
Full Squat Position
As the sandbag approaches the chest at the end of the liftoff
phase, drop the hips rapidly into a deep squat position so that
the sandbag is tightly held against the chest and resting on the
upper thighs.
Once secure and balanced in this position, initiate the
concentric phase of the squat movement by vigorously
extending the hip and knee joints while continuing to strongly
pull the sandbag into the chest.
Arms may be kept on the side of or above the sandbag,
depending on personal preference.
Carrying Phase
From here, adopt a walking movement pattern, initially focusing
on performing a series of short, fast steps, which will increase
total body balance and stability.
When building up speed, increase the length of the steps and
further reduce ground contact times.
