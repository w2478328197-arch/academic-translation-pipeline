sessions should be prearranged, and therefore athletes must have
an approved training sheet when training. Additional sessions should
be approved by the overseeing strength and conditioning
professional to ensure proper intensity and volume-load monitoring.
Strength and conditioning staff members must be familiar with every
exercise on each athlete’s training sheet and should not advise an
athlete to perform other exercises unless stipulated by the director
or the sports medicine staff, which should be committed to writing
as soon as possible after the session. For each exercise that is
included on the sport teams’ training sheets, including plyometric,
agility, and speed development drills, the director needs to inform
the staff regarding how the exercise technique should be taught to
athletes and also provide demonstrations and instruction. For
additional supervision, strength and conditioning professionals may
consider requiring the athletes’ last set of foundational exercises to
be seen by a staff member and checked off for proper execution.
Establishing consistent verbiage across performance training
programs is recommended.
