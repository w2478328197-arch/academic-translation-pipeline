Sympathetic Overtraining Syndrome
For a number of years, the existence of a sympathetic overtraining
syndrome was hypothetical, until it was shown in a group of
resistance exercise overtrained subjects (36). It had been
hypothesized that a sympathetic overtraining syndrome would occur
before development of a parasympathetic overtraining syndrome,
but enhanced sympathetic activity had never been previously shown
in response to an OT stimulus. The primary characteristic of
sympathetic overtraining syndrome is an increased epinephrine
response to exercise and training (36, 37, 38, 40, 41, 139).
Eventually, the elevated catecholamines result in a down-
regulation of their primary receptor in skeletal muscle, β-2
receptors (41, 124). This is believed to be an important part of the
sympathetic overtraining syndrome and the accompanying impaired
contractility of skeletal muscle in this condition.
