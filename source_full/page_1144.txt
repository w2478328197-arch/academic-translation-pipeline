Kneel facing the stability ball with the upper body in an upright
position, the elbows fully extended, and the hands touching the
ball.
While keeping the hands on the ball, create a 90-degree angle
at the knees and ankles with the knees, hips, and shoulders in a
near-vertical plane.
Ending Position
Keep the knees and toes on the floor; elbows fully extended;
arms parallel to each other; and knees, hips, and shoulders in a
straight line.
Extend the knees and flex the shoulders to roll the ball forward
and the arms across the top of the ball until it comes very close
to the face.
Isometrically hold the torso in a rigid position; do not let the
hips sag toward the floor.
MAJOR MUSCLES INVOLVED
rectus abdominis, iliopsoas

Starting Position
