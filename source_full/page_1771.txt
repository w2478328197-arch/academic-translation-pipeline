Wooden platforms should be free of splinters, cracks, holes, protruding nails,
loose screws, and warping.
Tighten bolts and screws and ensure they are secure in attaching equipment
to the floor.
Tile flooring should be resistant to slipping; no moisture or chalk
accumulation should appear.
Rubber flooring should be slip resistant and free of cuts, slits, and gaps
between pieces.
Interlocking mats are secure and arranged with no protruding tabs.
Carpet should be free of tears; any worn areas should be protected by mats.
Flooring and turf are glued or fastened down properly.
Walls
Walls in high-activity areas are free of protruding appliances, equipment, or
wall hangings.
Mirrors and shelves are securely fixed to walls.
Mirrors are placed a minimum of 20 inches (51 cm) off the floor in all areas.
Mirrors and windows are not cracked, broken, or distorted (replace
immediately if damaged).
Bolts and screws are tightened and secure in attaching any balance railing to
the walls.
Wall hooks and suspension anchors are secure for use with attachments.
Ceiling
Ceiling fans, HVAC, and air exchange systems work properly.
Lighting is designed to optimize visibility at floor level.
Ceiling height is sufficient for overhead exercises (12 ft [3.7 m] minimum)
and free of low-hanging apparatus (e.g., beams, pipes, lighting, signs).
Ceiling fixtures and attachments are secure, preventing the risk of falling
objects.
Any damaged or missing ceiling tiles are replaced as soon as possible.
Exercise Equipment
Stretching and Bodyweight Exercise Area
Mat area is free of weight benches and equipment.
Mats and bench upholstery are free of cracks and tears.
Equipment is properly stored after use.
Elastic bands are secured to their base and checked for wear.
