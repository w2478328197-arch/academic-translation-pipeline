Skill level and complexity. Exercise classification should also
consider the skill level and complexity involved in performing a
particular movement. Basic compound movements like squats,
deadlifts, and presses may serve as foundational tests for
assessing general strength and movement proficiency.
Conversely, more advanced or sport-specific movements
require greater neuromuscular coordination and may be
included to evaluate athletic prowess in specific contexts.
