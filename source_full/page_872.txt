Guidelines and Precautions for CNS
Preparatory Exercises
The stimulatory effect is likely greater with well-trained athletes with high
speed and power capacities.
Resistance-based protocols likely have the greatest potential for gain but also
the greatest potential for fatigue.
Plyometric and resisted sprint-based exercises may have lower potential but
be more accessible for use in warm-ups.
Higher intensities will likely generate greater potentiation but also greater
fatigue and thus require greater recovery times.
The closer the similarity between the preconditioning activity and the main
activity, the higher the likely transfer to enhanced performance.
The effects of all protocols will be highly variable and should be prescribed on
an individual basis.
Some athletes may not benefit from CNS stimulation.
The logistical challenges of implementing protocols may outweigh any
potential benefits.
Any decision should be based on a wide and comprehensive cost-benefit
analysis.
