Upward movement: Explosively jump forward and up, using both
arms to assist, with a goal of achieving maximal horizontal distance.
Downward movement: Dorsiflex the ankles in preparation for an
active flat-foot landing. Land on both feet. Allow recovery time
between jumps.
Note: This is typically a single ballistic effort, with several repetitions
in a set. However, it can be done as multiple jumps in sequence.
Care should be taken so that the forward momentum of the athlete
does not cause overrotation in the sagittal plane. Coach the athlete
to jump up and out; otherwise the tendency is for too much
horizontal momentum for the athlete to control.
COMMON ERRORS
Inefficient takeoff angle, resulting in suboptimal flight
distance; the lean should be far enough forward to
optimize horizontal propulsion but not so far that the
center of mass is too anteriorly located to optimize
ground reaction forces
Lack of full extension in the legs and full flexion in the
shoulders at takeoff, which reduces the opportunity for
ground reaction impulse to be created
