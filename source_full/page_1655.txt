been classified as a more functional form of exercise than open

example, during the closed kinetic chain squat exercise, the feet are
“fixed” to the floor and essentially do not move, providing a base on
which movement occurs (figure 23.5a). Closed kinetic chain
exercises have several advantages, including increased joint stability
and functional movement patterns; during sport activity, joints are
not typically used in isolation but rather work in concert with the
adjacent joints and surrounding musculature. Although closed kinetic
chain exercises are commonly viewed as lower extremity exercises,
closed chain upper extremity exercises exist as well (figure 23.5b)
(57, 100).
