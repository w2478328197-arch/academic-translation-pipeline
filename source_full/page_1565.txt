Adjust the handlebars so that when the arms are extended at a
downward angle the elbows are slightly flexed. Ideally, the
lower arm and torso will form an angle of approximately 90
degrees.
Movement Phase
Begin pedaling while keeping the balls of the feet in contact
with the pedals throughout the duration of the exercise.
Maintain a neutral spine posture and do not round the
shoulders.
With bullhorn handlebars, a variety of hand positions may be
used. These include
pronated, palms-facing-downward grip, allowing a more
upright posture;
neutral palms-facing grip on the sides of the handlebars,
encouraging a greater forward lean; and
racing position, with the forearms resting on the handlebars,
creating maximum forward lean.
Ending Position
Slow down until the pedals have come to a complete stop, and
then step off the bike.
COMMON ERRORS
If pedaling using the toes only and not the balls of the
feet or moving laterally in the seat, the seat is too high.
If the knees come above the height of the hips at the top
of the pedaling stroke, the seat is too low.
If struggling to reach the handlebars, the seat may be
too far back or the handlebars too low. Correct seat and
handlebar height ensures maintenance of power output
throughout the pedaling stroke.
