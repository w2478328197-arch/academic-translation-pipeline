Since most activities with a kettlebell are dynamic and require space
to complete a variety of movements, this section should be large
enough to accommodate both the exercises being performed and
the number of athletes using the space simultaneously.
Weightlifting Area
A weightlifting area generally contains racks with platforms or a
platform standing alone. It could also be an open area where
weightlifting is performed without a platform. This is typically done
on a rubber floor with a concrete foundation. To keep things
organized, weight trees or racks and bar holders can be used when
the equipment is not in use.
Racks and platforms should have enough room between them
that if someone were to fall, people nearby would not be injured.
Weightlifting racks should be bolted to the floor to ensure that they
do not move when in use. If a rack is portable, it should be moved
to a designated storage area when not in use.
Exercise and Performance Testing Spaces
Exercise testing equipment should be in their own section or
dedicated space. Exercise testing spaces are where equipment is
grouped together for exercise testing sessions. This equipment may
consist of force plates, isometric mid-thigh pulling racks, exercise
testing bikes and treadmills, testing and monitoring device charging
stations, and associated computer equipment. Ensuring a consistent
location for exercise testing within the training facility is optimal for
ensuring the validity and reliability of exercise testing.
It is also important to consider safety in implementing technology
devices that are specifically designed for integration into free weight
and weightlifting areas. Such equipment includes rack-mounted
training feedback tablets, barbell attachments, linear position
transducers, accelerometers, and mounted camera systems. As a
general rule, weight room technology should be set up and
administered in a way that does not impede planned safety space
