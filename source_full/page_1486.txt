  2   A-SKIP
The A-skip is a commonly prescribed sprint drill to develop the
correct leg recovery mechanics for maximum-velocity sprint running.
Starting Position
Begin the exercise in a tall stance with the torso directly above
the hips, knees, and ankles (stacked joints).
Movement Phase
Initiate movement by lifting one leg to a flexed knee position in
which the top of the thigh is near parallel. The foot of the lifted
leg (swing leg) should be approximately at knee height of the
stance leg, with the heel pulled up under the buttocks and the
ankle dorsiflexed. This will result in the appearance of a figure
four at the legs. The swing leg will always accord with this
description.
Begin the first skip by driving the swing leg down to the ground
through an active foot, established through slight dorsiflexion.
Lift the big toe up to establish appropriate dorsiflexion.
