Warm-Up
As in any training program, a plyometric exercise session should
begin with a specific warm-up that incorporates activation,
mobilization, 
and 
potentiation 
of 
the 
neuromuscular 
and
musculoskeletal system (refer to chapter 15 for movement
preparation guidelines). The specific warm-up for plyometric training
should consist of low-intensity, dynamic movements that increase in
intensity as the warm-up progresses. Table 19.7 provides a list and
explanation of examples of specific warm-up drills for plyometric
training.
Steps for Implementing a Plyometric
Training Program
1. Evaluate the athlete, including the athlete’s sport and training history.
2. Establish sport-, position-, and athlete-specific goals.
3. Teach the athlete proper jumping, landing, and throwing technique.
4. Assign proper plyometric training program design variables, addressing
intensity, frequency, recovery, and volume.
5. Properly progress the plyometric training program.
