Inspect all protective padding for cracks and tears.
Inspect nonslip material and mats for proper placement, damage, and wear.
Clean and sanitize exercise mats.
Remove trash and garbage.
Ensure equipment is returned and stored properly after use.
Two or Three Times Per Week
Clean and lubricate all moving parts of equipment and aerobic machines.
Clean and lubricate the guide rods on selectorized resistance training
machines.
Ensure Olympic bars turn properly and are properly lubricated and tightened.
Once Per Week or Two
Clean and dust ceiling fixtures and attachments.
Clean ceiling tiles.
Clean walls.
Clean light covers, fans, air vents, clocks, and speakers.
As Needed
Replace light bulbs.
Replace damaged or missing ceiling tiles.
Clean open ceilings with exposed pipes or ducts.
Remove or place “out of order” sign on broken equipment.
Fill chalk boxes.
Clean bar knurling.
Remove rust from floor, plates, bars, and equipment with a rust-removing
solution.
Wash and sanitize rags and gym towels per use.
From National Strength and Conditioning Association, Essentials of Strength
Training and Conditioning, 5th ed., edited by G.G. Haff and N.T. Triplett (Human
