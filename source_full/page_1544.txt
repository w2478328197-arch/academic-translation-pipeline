Off-Season (Base Training)
The priority in off-season training is to develop a large base of
cardiorespiratory fitness. Initially, the training program should
progress to be composed of long-duration and low-intensity
workouts. In the mid- to late off-season, intensity and, to a lesser
extent, duration are increased; however, it is typically recommended
that intermediate and advanced athletes should not increase their
training duration or volume more than 5% to 10% per week.
