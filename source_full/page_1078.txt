   44   CLEAN GRIP MID-THIGH PULL
(AND SNATCH GRIP VARIATION)
The performance of the mid-thigh pull with the clean or snatch grip
requires the use of lifting blocks or a rack that allows the loaded
barbell to be positioned so that the athlete initiates the movement
from the power position (i.e., second pull position, knee angle ~125-
145 degrees and hip angle ~140-150 degrees). When using the
clean grip, the bar will generally be positioned across the upper
thigh, depending on the athlete’s individual anthropometrics (i.e.,
arm length). If the snatch grip is used, the bar will generally be
higher on the thigh and in most instances sit across the hip crease
as a result of the wider grip. To accommodate the wider grip used
during the snatch grip mid-thigh pull, the loaded bar will need to be
placed on higher blocks (or rack position) than what is used with the
clean grip mid-thigh pull. While there are differences in the
placement of the barbell between the clean and snatch grip variants
of this lift the fundamental movement patterns associated with
performing the mid-thigh pull are the same regardless of which grip
is used.
Starting Position
The power position is the starting position for the clean grip mid-
thigh pull (and snatch grip mid-thigh pull).
Stand with the feet placed between hip- and shoulder width
apart with the toes pointed slightly outward.
Grasp the bar with a clean grip (i.e., slightly wider than
shoulder-width apart) while using lifting straps with the elbows
fully extended and pointing outward (i.e., arms internally
rotated and the shoulder retracted). (If performing this exercise
with a snatch grip, grasp the bar with a wider grip based on the
grip established for the power snatch.)
