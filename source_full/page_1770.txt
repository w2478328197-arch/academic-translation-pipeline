Figure 25.5  Checklist for cleaning floors, walls,
ceilings, and surfaces.
Floors
Check floors for dirt, grime, and chalk buildup.
Nonabsorbent floors should be regularly swept and mopped.
Check carpet for any moisture, mold, mildew, or fungal growth.
Carpet should be vacuumed and cleaned regularly.
Walls
Check walls for dirt and dust buildup.
Dust and clean walls at least once every week or two.
Clean mirrors and windows of smudges at least once per week.
Ceilings
Ceilings and ceiling fixtures should be dusted or cleaned at least once every
week or two.
Open ceilings with exposed pipes and ducts should be cleaned as needed.
Check for dust and cobweb buildup (especially near corners and HVAC
vents).
Surfaces
Dust windowsills and shelving surfaces at least once per week.
Exercise surfaces, mats, and pads that contact skin should be cleaned daily
with germicidal cleaner.
Clean and sanitize hydration and nutrition spaces (drinking fountains, water
bottle refill stations, countertops) daily and additionally as needed.
From National Strength and Conditioning Association, Essentials of Strength
Training and Conditioning, 5th ed., edited by G.G. Haff and N.T. Triplett (Human
Kinetics, 2027).
