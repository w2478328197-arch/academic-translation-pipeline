Downward movement: Rotate over the hurdle so the lean is in the
direction of the subsequent jump on landing. Dorsiflex the ankles in
preparation for an active flat-foot landing on the opposite side of the
barrier, and immediately repeat the jump to the starting side. (Note:
The third photo does not show the athlete leaning back toward the
starting side for the return jump.)
Note: The intensity level of the lateral barrier hop can be increased
from medium to high by progressively increasing the height of the
barrier or by performing movement with one leg only (i.e., a hop),
which is an excellent exercise for aiding direction change capacity in
the athlete. By reversing the direction of movement, the ground
contact time and the amortization phase of the movement are
changed compared to movements in the same direction. This will be
influenced further by the height of the hurdle.
COMMON ERRORS
Not maintaining a square hip and shoulder position, so
the jump does not stay in the frontal plane
Lack of preparatory lean in both takeoff (center of mass
should lean in the direction of travel) and landing (orient
the body so that it is leaning back toward the start for
the return jump), which increases the ground contact
time and reduces the effectiveness of the drill
