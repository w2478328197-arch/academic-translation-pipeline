Forward Pushing Phase
While bracing the spine, initiate a stepping pattern that initially
focuses on performing a series of short, relatively fast steps,
which will increase the ability to apply horizontal forces to the
ground.
While building up speed, increase the length of the steps and
further reduce ground contact times. The ability to apply
propulsive horizontal forces to the ground can be optimized by
ensuring the front foot is positioned behind the hip throughout
the entire pushing phase.
Starting Position (Pull)
Stand in front of the sled and attach the sled to the body via a
chest and shoulder harness (shown) or hold separate handles in
each hand (not shown).
Regardless of the attachment method to the sled, assume a
position in which the trunk and shin are close to horizontal (not
shown), which will increase the ability to apply propulsive
horizontal forces and accelerate the sled. This can be best
achieved by placing the hands on the ground (not shown) if
connected via a harness to the sled or by leaning forward (not
shown) if holding handles in each hand.
