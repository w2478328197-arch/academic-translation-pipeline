Training Area
The amount of space needed depends on the drill. Most bounding
and running drills require at least 30 yards (or meters) of
straightaway, though some drills may require a straightaway of 100
yards (or meters). For most standing, box, and depth jumps, only a
minimal surface area is needed, but the ceiling height must be at
least 10 feet (3 m) to be adequate.
Equipment
Boxes used for box jumps and depth jumps must be sturdy and
should have a nonslip top. Boxes should range in height from 6 to 42
inches (15-107 cm) and should have landing surfaces of at least 18
by 24 inches (46 × 61 cm) (19). The box should be constructed of
sturdy wood (e.g., 3/4-inch [1.9 cm] plywood) or heavy-gauge metal
that is covered in a protective covering to reduce the risk of injury if
the athlete contacts the box. High density foam within a cubed
framework with solid landing surfaces is also appropriate. To further
reduce injury risk, there are several ways of making the landing
surface nonslip such as adding nonslip treads, mixing sand into the
paint used to cover the box, or affixing rubberized flooring to the top
(19).
Proper Footwear
Athletes must use footwear with good ankle and foot support; good
lateral stability; and a wide, nonslip sole (50). Ankle injury risk may
be increased in shoes with a narrow sole and poor mid-foot support,
especially with lateral movements. Shoes with insufficient foot
