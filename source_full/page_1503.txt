Y agility drills can be further progressed using generic or sport-
specific stimuli. For example, the drill can be adapted whereby
the athletes must react to auditory or visual stimuli when
approaching the turning area, such as a color cone or auditory
command (i.e., left or right). Moreover, the tasks can be further
developed by incorporating a sport-specific stimuli such as an
opponent and alternating between leading (attacking) and
following (defending) through the use of mirror drills.
