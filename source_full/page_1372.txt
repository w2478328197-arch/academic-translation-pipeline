Equipment: Medicine or plyometric ball weighing 8 to 20 pounds (4-
9 kg); wall or partner
Starting position: Get into a comfortable, upright stance with feet
hip-width apart; face the wall or partner approximately 10 feet (3 m)
away. Raise the ball overhead.
Preparatory movement: Begin with a countermovement (i.e., cock
the arms by moving them slightly backward before the throw).
Arm action: Using both arms, throw the ball to the wall or partner,
keeping the elbows extended. When the wall or partner returns the
ball, catch the ball and immediately repeat the throw. The partners
can also bounce the ball on the ground between them for a
downward movement and a catch on the rebound.
COMMON ERRORS
Arching the back in the start position, increasing lumbar
spine involvement (rather than hip flexion) in the
throwing action
Not coordinating or sequencing downward throwing
action so the optimal horizontal distance is not achieved
