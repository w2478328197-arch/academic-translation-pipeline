A major challenge to understanding overtraining and its causes
and effects are the numerous different terms found in the literature.
This is one reason several groups have developed position stands
(88, 89) and consensus statements (65) that include suggested
terminology. Likewise, many symptoms of an overtraining syndrome
can vary between individuals and between different types of training.
However, it is important to note that in all cases, performance is
impaired in some manner. Figure 24.5 lists a number of the terms
used in the literature for overtraining and its effects.
