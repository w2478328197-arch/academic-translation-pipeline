Electrical Service and Devices
A strength and conditioning facility typically requires more outlets
than other buildings. Some outlets may require relatively higher
voltage for more powerful equipment, such as stair climbers,
elliptical trainers, and treadmills. The electrical service also needs to
be properly grounded to protect the system from lightning strikes or
power surges. Ground fault circuit interrupter (GFCI) outlets are also
necessary to ensure safety in the event of an electrical short (1).
With the increase of technology now being used in weight rooms,
the placement of wall, floor, and elevated power outlets should be
considered to limit future issues with devices and power cords once
the facility becomes operational. Specifically, power cords should
never extend into exercise areas, safety spacing, walkways, or
spotter areas. In addition, any equipment and device charging
stations should be in an office or storage area away from weight
room traffic. If televisions will be used in the facility, the use of
elevated outlets and secured wall or ceiling mounts are
recommended. Planning ahead for the use of technology is valuable,
especially if the intent is to use television monitors or overhead
projectors to communicate workouts and announcements.
Walls and Mirrors
Mirrors can be used for many purposes in a strength and
conditioning facility. They can be used as a coaching tool if racks and
platforms are placed in the right position, because they provide
immediate visual feedback for improved spatial awareness and
safety (14). Mirrors can also be used to enhance a room
aesthetically.
