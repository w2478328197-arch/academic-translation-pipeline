applied load is reduced, because the bands will no longer be
stretched and thus no longer apply resistance to the barbell. For
example, in the squat at the lowest point (i.e., bottom position,
figure 17.3), the bands are slack and do not actively create tension
on the barbell, thus adding no additional resistance to the exercise.
As the athlete ascends from the bottom position and approaches the
lockout position, the bands will impart a larger stretch resistance,
and the athlete will experience the full load of both the bar and the
bands (figure 17.4). It is important to note that as the athlete
ascends from the bottom position the stretch load gradually
increases. Conversely, as the athlete descends, the stretch load
gradually decreases.
