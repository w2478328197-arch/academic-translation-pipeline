Remember that GH will be secreted from two different types (I and II) of anterior pituitary
somatotrophs, each releasing different molecular weight isoforms.
Resistance exercise increases the 22 kD isoform in both men and
women. It is sensitive to the amount of total work performed and
the rest period duration used because these affect the demands on
the glycolytic energy system and H+ ion production; drops in pH are
a key stimulator of 22 GH release (41, 54). Women have been
shown to have higher resting values of the 22 kD isoform of GH than
men in the early follicular phase of the menstrual cycle (81). GH
levels in women tend to be higher during the menstrual cycle’s
follicular phase than in the luteal phase. During the follicular phase,
which occurs from the beginning of menstruation until ovulation,
estrogen levels rise, and this hormonal environment is associated
with increased GH secretion. While GH is present throughout the
entire cycle, its secretion is generally more pronounced during this
phase due to the influence of estrogen, which stimulates GH
production. Additionally, oral contraceptive use can influence
women’s acute GH response to exercise (84). Specifically, women
