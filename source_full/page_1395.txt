Preparatory movement: Brace the core. Breathe in and pull the ribs
down toward the hips to tighten the core musculature to prevent the
lumbar spine from going into forced extension during the movement.
Drill action: The athlete raises the legs toward the partner’s
outstretched hands, and the partner briefly grasps the athlete’s
lower legs before forcefully pushing the athlete’s legs down toward
the ground. Before the legs reach the ground, the athlete
decelerates and ultimately stops the downward movement before
flexing the hips to return to the starting position to reset for the next
repetition. Immediately repeat the movement.
Note: Intensity may be increased by using greater force of the
downward push or by altering the direction of the push so that the
leg action is not purely in the sagittal plane.
COMMON ERRORS
Lowering the legs without waiting for the partner to
push them to the ground, reducing the eccentric load of
the exercise
Not controlling the lumbopelvic position and extending
the spine as the legs are lowered, increasing the
pressure on the lower back musculature
