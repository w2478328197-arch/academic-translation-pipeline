The trunk leans forward slightly with the head up and eyes
looking forward. The athlete adopts a contralateral arm
movement at a lower amplitude of maximal sprint running, but
the timing and range of motion should match the timing and
range of motion of the legs.
Technique Tips
Emphasize the circular recovery motion of the foot.
Maintain a slight trunk lean with head up and eyes looking
forward.
Ensure the contralateral arm movement matches the timing and
range of motion of the leg action.
Regressions and Progressions
If the athlete initially struggles with the ankle dribble, he or she
could lower the intensity to a walking pace and work on the
circular path of movement of the foot over the opposing ankle
using a heel–toe contact.
Progression from the ankle dribble involves greater range of
movement of the circular foot pattern, so the foot first reaches
calf height (calf dribble) and then knee height (knee dribble).
Furthermore, after mastering these versions, progressions could
involve dribble bleeds whereby the athlete may progress from
ankle to calf to knee dribble within the same repetition or blend
a knee dribble to a high-speed run.
