Upward movement: Forcefully triple extend to jump onto the top of
the box using both legs.
Downward movement: Land on both feet in a half-squat position,
step down from the box, and repeat.
Note: The arm action contributes significantly to the upward
momentum of this jump. By having athletes perform the movement
with their hands on their hips, or their fingers interlocked behind the
head and elbows pointing sideways, the lower body power required
to perform the jump can be significantly increased.
COMMON ERRORS
Focusing on landing on the box and flexing hips and
knees early, thus reducing vertical power; the aim should
be to land on the box because of powerful extension into
the ground, maximizing the forward and upward
movement of the body
Not coordinating arm actions with the forward
movement of the body; powerful shoulder flexion should
be simultaneous with the lower body jumping action
