What Is a Board Certified Specialist in
Sports Dietetics (CSSD)?
According to the Commission on Dietetic Registration, the credentialing agency for
the Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics, a CSSD “provides individual and
group/team counseling and education to enhance the performance of competitive
and recreational athletes.” Their responsibilities include:
Counseling individuals and groups on daily nutrition for performance and
health
Translating the latest scientific evidence into practical sports nutrition
recommendations
Tracking and documenting outcomes of nutrition services, serving as a food
and nutrition resource for coaches, support staff, and families
Some of the tasks that an individual with a CSSD certification can conduct
according to the CSSD exam content outline include:
Interpreting body composition assessments on active individuals
Developing appropriate body composition goals
Estimating total energy expenditure
Assessing fluid and electrolyte balance in training, performance, and recovery
Evaluating and educating on dietary supplements, beverages, ergogenic aids
using evidence-based analyses for training, performance, hydration, and
recovery
Contributing to collaborative interventions for and monitoring of disordered
eating to guide sport participation

At times, physicians and sports dietitians may work together to
help athletes with eating disorders, nutrient deficiencies, or specific
disease states such as diabetes. Because an athlete’s nutrition and
medical information may be shared with other members of the
player development or coaching staff or family members in order to
provide comprehensive care, in the United States all staff should
follow Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA)
guidelines for handling an athlete’s protected health information.
