Strength and conditioning professionals can view psychological
distress in many ways. One method is for coaches to document
issues in mental health through identifying signs and symptoms
(table 9.1). Signs are observable outcomes that can be readily
assessed by the coach. For instance, a sign of depression is the
lessening of effort observed by the coach. Symptoms are usually
self-reported feelings or sensations. The symptoms would be the
athlete reporting that he or she feels tired or has less power.
