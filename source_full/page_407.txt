From a clinical perspective, it has been reported that muscular
fitness is associated with lower obesity, insulin resistance, and
cardiovascular risk among children and adolescents (43, 84). Of
note, meta-analytical data indicate that resistance training can
reduce body fat in children and adolescents who are overweight or
obese (153). The grades for indicators of youth physical activity
around the world are low, which suggests a widespread crisis of
physical inactivity (6). Children who are obese or those who lead
sedentary lives (e.g., who take a bus to school and watch television
or play computer games after school and on weekends) are not
ready for 1 to 2 hours of sport training four or five days a week.

enjoy resistance training because it is not aerobically taxing and
provides an opportunity for all participants to experience success
and feel good about their performance.
