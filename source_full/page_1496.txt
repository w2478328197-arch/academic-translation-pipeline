The athlete should adopt a contralateral limb movement that
matches the timing and range of motion of the limbs.
Technique Tips
Maintain an upright trunk without leaning back.
Emphasize rapid, forceful retraction of the limb toward ground
contact, and ensure that ground contacts are short and under
the hips.
Adopt a contralateral arm movement that matches the timing
and range of motion of the lower limbs.
Regressions and Progressions
If the athlete initially struggles with the straight-leg bound, the
athlete could lower the intensity by reducing the hip flexion
range of motion and speed of retraction, and gradually and
progressively build up the speed and range of motion.
Trunk stability and lumbopelvic control can be challenged with
overhead variations of straight-leg bounds, and straight-leg
bounds can be transitioned or blended into normal upright
running with the aim that previous straight-leg bounds will
transfer to increased hip angular velocities, limb retraction, and
active touchdowns with high velocities (i.e., 20-yard straight leg
build up into flying 20-yard sprint).
