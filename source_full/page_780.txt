FIGURE 14.4 Isometric mid-thigh pull (IMTP) test position.
Procedure
1. The testing rack needs to be set to a height that allows the athlete to mirror the “second pull”
position used in weightlifting (i.e., clean or power clean). In this position the immovable bar is
positioned across the upper portion of the thigh, immediately inferior to the pelvis (58) (figure
14.4).
2. When performing the test, the athlete should have an upright trunk position (62, 63) with the
feet roughly hip-width apart and a slight flex of the knee while grasping the bar with a clean
grip while using weightlifting straps (58). In this position the knee angle should be
approximately 120 degrees and 145 degrees, and the hip angle should be approximately 125
degrees and 150 degrees (62). However, it is important to note that the exact bar position as
well as knee and hip angles are difficult to recommend because an individual’s anthropometrics
will exert a large influence on the exact position during the test.
3. Before conducting the isometric mid-thigh pull test it is important that between one and three
familiarization sessions are performed in order to enhance the reliability of the test (62). Less
familiarization is needed with trained athletes, especially those who train regularly with
weightlifting derivatives. First, the athlete should complete a dynamic warm-up that includes
dynamic stretches (e.g., bodyweight squats and lunges) as well as dynamic lifting tasks (e.g.,
low-load and moderate-load mid-thigh pulls). After this is completed, a series of progressively
intense isometric actions should be performed at perceived intensities of 50%, 70%, and 90%
