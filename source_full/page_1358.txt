Preparatory movement: Step from the box.
Downward movement: Dorsiflex the ankles and land on the ground
with an active flat-foot contact with both feet.
Upward movement: On landing, immediately jump up as high as
possible.
Note: When stepping from the box, step straight out. Do not first
jump up or lower the center of gravity while stepping down, because
these adjustments will change the height from which the exercise is
performed. The time on the ground should be kept to a minimum;
there is an optimal relationship between ground contact time and
resultant vertical force production. On landing, emphasis should be
on jumping up, with minimal horizontal movement (less than what is
shown in the third photo).
COMMON ERRORS
Absorbing ground reaction force as the athlete lands
through hip and knee flexion, which increases the
amortization phase and ground contact time; hips,
knees, and ankles should be stiff when landing to
optimize the ground contact time and rebound jump
height
Not dorsiflexing the ankle in preparation for ground
contact; this negates the active flat-foot contact and
increases ground contact time
