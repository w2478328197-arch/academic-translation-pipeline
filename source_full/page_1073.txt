   43   POWER SNATCH (AND HANG
POWER SNATCH VARIATION)
This exercise consists of quickly and forcefully pulling the bar from
the floor to a position overhead with the elbows fully extended.
Although the ascent consists of four phases (i.e., first pull, transition,
second pull, and catch), the upward movement of the bar occurs in
one continuous motion. While the power snatch can be initiated
from the floor, it can also be initiated from a hang position (i.e.,
power position or slightly above or below the knee). When a hang
power snatch is performed it is important to note that the bar is not
returned to the floor or rack between repetitions. Regardless of
where the power clean is initiated from, the bar is received in a
position where the top of the thighs are above parallel.
