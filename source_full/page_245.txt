FIGURE 4.3 A simplified model of steroid hormone action. The figure illustrates how
steroid hormones like testosterone in skeletal muscle or dihydrotestosterone in sex-linked
tissues migrate into target cells. Although the pathways for both hormones are represented,
each targets only one specific cell type but they are shown together for clarity. Each
hormone pathway leads to different physiological outcomes, emphasizing the selective
nature of steroid–hormone interactions.
Figure 4.4 depicts how a typical polypeptide hormone might
interact with the cell nucleus through the cytokine-activated Janus
kinase (JAK)/signal transducer and activator of the transcription
(STAT) signaling pathway. The JAK/STAT pathway transmits signals
from various polypeptide hormones into the cell. However, like
testosterone, polypeptide hormones may also have nongenomic
actions. For instance, GH stimulates lipolysis, thus sparing muscle
glycogen during and after a workout (110).
