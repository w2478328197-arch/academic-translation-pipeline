Stress
Stress is defined as a substantial imbalance between demand
(physical, psychological, or both) and perceived response capability,
under conditions in which failure to meet that demand has important
consequences (51). A stressor is an environmental or cognitive
event that precipitates stress (i.e., the stress response). Stress can
be described as a negative (distress) or a positive (eustress)
state. Both of these generate arousal but only when the perception
of the stressor is negative—distress—does it also generate anxiety.
Therefore, distress comprises cognitive and somatic anxiety, whereas
eustress comprises positive mental energy and physiological arousal.
