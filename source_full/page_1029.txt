Backward Movement Phase: Athlete
Take one exaggerated step directly backward with one leg.
Keep the torso erect as the rear foot moves backward and
contacts the floor.
Keep the front foot in the starting position, but allow the
backward knee to flex toward the floor.
Keep the lead foot flat on the floor pointing straight ahead.
Allow the front hip and knee to flex slowly.
Keep the front knee directly over the front foot.
Continue to flex the front knee until the rear knee is 1 to 2
inches (3-5 cm) above the floor.
Balance the weight evenly between the ball of the trailing foot
and the whole lead foot.
