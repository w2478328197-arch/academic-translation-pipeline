Fat Oxidation
The oxidative energy system can alternatively use fats. Triglycerides
stored in fat cells can be broken down by an enzyme, hormone-
sensitive lipase, to produce free fatty acids and glycerol. The
breakdown releases a portion of the total free fatty acids from the
fat cells into the blood, where they can circulate and enter muscle
fibers and undergo oxidation (89, 123). Additionally, limited
quantities of triglycerides are stored within the muscle along with a
form of hormone-sensitive lipase to produce an intramuscular source
of free fatty acids (22, 47). Free fatty acids enter the mitochondria,
