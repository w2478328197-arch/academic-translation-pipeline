Benefits Associated With Accentuated Eccentric
Loading
The primary benefits associated with the use of AEL occur as a result
of an overload applied during the eccentric phase of a resistance
training or jumping exercise (91). Relatively little research has
explored the chronic application of AEL, but due to the greater
mechanical tension (43) and work (142, 143) during the eccentric
phase, some researchers have suggested that AEL would be useful
when targeting hypertrophy. While this rationale is sound, based on
the available research increases in muscle size associated with AEL
are comparable to traditional training methods (78, 119). It is,
however, plausible that AEL results in architectural changes, such as
the elongation of the muscle fascicles, allowing for higher shortening
velocities which could affect strength and power performance (91).
Additionally, the application of AEL tactics may have further benefits
to enhancing strength and power performance by inducing changes
to the neuromuscular system (144). This hypothesis is partially
supported by the work of Walker and colleagues (144), who have
reported improved voluntary muscle activation and significantly
greater improvements in maximal strength after 10 weeks of training
with AEL when compared to traditional resistance training.
Ultimately, while there is an emerging body of evidence that using
AEL tactics stimulates greater improvements in maximal strength
(52, 144) and jump performance (141), more research is needed to
determine the most effective AEL programming strategies.
