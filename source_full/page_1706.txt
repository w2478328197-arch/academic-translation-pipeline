Physiological Mechanisms Associated
With Overreaching and Overtraining
As discussed earlier, not all forms of overtraining and the resulting
overtraining syndrome are identical. Furthermore, the symptoms and
physiological responses can also be quite different depending on the
individual and the characteristics of the training program. However, a
number of physiological systems have either been shown or
theorized to be affected by overtraining. It should be noted that
although these mechanisms can be the result of an overtraining
stimulus, it is also possible that if these conditions are preexisting,
they can also contribute to an overtraining syndrome and be
considered part of the allostatic load. Understanding the underlying
physiology can, in some cases, provide insight on preventive
strategies to help minimize the risk of developing overtraining
syndrome and the accompanying impairments in performance. Table

mechanisms, as well as the strength of the data supporting each.
