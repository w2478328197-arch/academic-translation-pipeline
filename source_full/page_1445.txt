Technical Guidelines and Coaching
In comparison to sprinting, COD and agility have a large degree of
freedom due to the multitude of movements that occur during a
COD. Further, agility performance as restricted or determined by
opponents or other tactical restraints and scenarios cannot be
trained through the use of a single technique, with evidence to
suggest we should be creating within- and between-skill variability
and a flexible coordinative approach (35). Nevertheless, the
following are some technical guidelines and coaching suggestions.
Visual Focus
When changing direction in response to an opponent (either
offensive or defensive), the athlete should visually scan the
environment and opponent while focusing on the shoulders,
trunk, and hip.
Following the anticipation of the event, unless deception is
intended, the athlete should quickly redirect attention to a new
area to help lead the transition of the body.
Body Position During Braking and Reacceleration
Control the trunk leading into the deceleration (decrease large
amounts of trunk motion) (80). When decelerating, posterior
trunk lean may assist in orienting the force vector posteriorly.
Through the stance phase, reorient the trunk and hips toward
the direction of intended travel to allow for a more effective
reacceleration (22) and to reduce the redirection demands for
a more effective position to produce force (35).
