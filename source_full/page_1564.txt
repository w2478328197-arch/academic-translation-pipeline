Ending Position
Reduce the speed of the treadmill and perform a 3- to 5-minute
cooldown to prevent blood pooling in the lower extremities and
improve venous return to the heart.
Step onto the platforms on either side of the belt and turn the
machine off.
COMMON ERRORS
Continually holding the handrails—holding the handrails
beyond the initial entry phase interrupts posture and
stride mechanics and limits core activation; after the
initial entry phase use a natural walking or running
stride pattern with an upright posture and free moving
arm swing, as if walking or running outside
Looking continuously down at the console, which affects
stride mechanics and posture and can cause neck
discomfort; keep the eyes focused forward, with short
glances to the console as needed to monitor display
information
  2   STATIONARY BIKE
Starting Position
Begin by adjusting the seat height so the knee of the extended
leg is slightly flexed (25-30 degrees) at the bottom of the pedal
stroke.
The foot of the down leg should be flat and parallel to the floor
with the balls of the feet in contact with the pedals.
Adjust the seat so the knee is over the center of the pedal on
the extended leg and the hips do not rock back and forth during
pedaling.
While maintaining a neutral spine position, lean slightly forward
at the hips.
