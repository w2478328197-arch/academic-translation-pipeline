Begin in the correct starting position (commonly called a
crouched start). First, split the stance position by placing the
dominant leg forward and dropping the back or swing leg by
one to two foot lengths.
The length of split between the drive and swing leg is largely
determined by the compromise between the (a) ability to
generate sufficient forces needed to overcome a lower center of
gravity and (b) comfort. In addition, the feet should be split in
such a manner that the front and back legs are in line with the
pelvis, so that no unnecessary twisting of the hips occurs.
Once the split stance has been established, drop the back knee
straight down so that the shank (shin) is nearer (more parallel)
to the ground. This aids in promoting the proper driving position
needed to begin acceleration.
With a tall and rigid torso, raise the arm that is opposite to the
front or drive leg to a position near or slightly above the
forehead. The hand should be spaced approximately 6 to 8
inches (15-20 cm) away from the forehead. The rear arm
(opposite to the rear or swing leg) should be pulled back to a
point at which the hand is near the lateral aspect of the
buttocks with an elbow angle that may range from 100 degrees
to 120 degrees. This position allows for the arm action to assist
in producing sufficient propulsive forces to overcome static
inertia.
On completion of setup, lean the entire body forward so that
60% of the body weight is on the front leg. Avoid breaking at
the waist during this shift. The athlete should continue to feel
balanced and stable.
Movement Phase
After body weight has been transferred, the athlete can initiate
the sprint through an aggressive push down and through the
ground via the front or drive leg.
