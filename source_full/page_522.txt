Progressive Integration Into Full-Group or Team
Activities
One of the most basic psychological needs to enhance intrinsic
motivation is relatedness, or a sense of belonging and connection
(46). Progressively moving from privately scheduled, one-on-one
rehabilitation sessions to larger team sessions may be an
appropriate tactic to enhance the connection between the athlete
and the team.
Improve Self-Efficacy and Motivation
Readiness to return to competition is a highly subjective and
complex process. Characteristics of athletes who are ready to return
to play demonstrate realistic expectations, high levels of self-efficacy,
and low levels of anxiety (18). Coaches should minimize
psychological barriers such as fear or anxiety by providing a
psychologically 
safe 
and 
autonomy-supportive 
environment.
Additionally, enhancing the athlete’s self-efficacy about specific tasks
(e.g., sprinting, jumping, cutting) and motivation to return to pre-
injury levels would create a more intrinsically driven, process-
oriented approach compared to an extrinsically driven, outcome-
oriented approach to return to play (63).
