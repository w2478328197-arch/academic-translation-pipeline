Monitoring Protocols, Procedures, and Equipment
When selecting tests for athletic assessment, it is crucial to consider the protocols, procedures, and
equipment necessary to effectively implement them. Every athletic environment is unique, requiring
careful consideration to optimize these aspects for specific settings.
Protocols are systematic plans outlining guidelines to achieve specific objectives. In athlete
testing, they provide a comprehensive framework within which procedures are developed, setting
standards for consistency, reliability, and validity (as explained in detail in chapter 13). A monitoring
protocol represents the overarching strategy for conducting performance assessments, detailing the
overall approach for tracking an athlete’s physical capacities and specifying test types and
frequencies throughout the season.
Using Artificial Intelligence to Analyze Testing Data
Artificial intelligence (AI) has emerged as a potentially transformative tool in sport. Yet there is still much confusion,
apprehension, and skepticism about the value of AI. AI is defined as “the ability of a digital computer to perform tasks
commonly associated with intelligent beings … such as the ability to reason, discover meaning, generalize, or learn from
past experience” (31). Within AI, machine learning (ML) is the ability of computer machines to learn from and develop
data without human input or interaction. These approaches offer various advantages to sport, including automating or
semiautomating data collection, processing data into meaningful insights, identifying crucial information related to health
and performance, and creating models that support complex decision making (40). Such models can handle a vast number
of factors and their interactions, which can be too complex for humans to process.
In the context of athlete testing, AI holds numerous potential applications. Its capacity to process large volumes of data
is particularly suited to biomechanical analysis, leading to its integration into a number of movement analysis solutions (12).
Moreover, AI can be used to streamline certain technologies and tests, making jump, speed, and velocity-based gym
exercise assessments feasible with just a mobile phone (9). By leveraging the quality of modern phone cameras and
applying AI and ML techniques to analyze the data, such tests become accessible to more strength and conditioning
professionals. However, it is crucial for practitioners to scrutinize the accuracy and validity of such technologies.
The use of AI in injury prediction is a widely debated subject (12), yet its potential to enhance existing approaches
cannot be overlooked. The ability of AI and ML to process vast quantities of data and comprehend complex interactions
within and across data sets may further such applications. Nevertheless, practitioners must ensure sufficient data collection
quantity and quality. As the maxim “garbage in, garbage out” implies, the success of any model is contingent on the quality
of its inputs. AI’s application in analyzing athlete testing data is a burgeoning field that holds promise for the future. Given
its rapid and constant evolution, it is imperative that practitioners stay informed of developments in this area.
Procedures are detailed, step-by-step instructions on executing specific tasks within the
protocol framework. They provide explicit guidance on methods to ensure consistent data
collection. In physical performance assessments, procedures detail the steps for conducting the
tests outlined in this chapter, such as vertical jumps, aerobic capacity, or agility performance,
ensuring standardized methodologies and reliable data.
Selecting appropriate equipment for athlete physical performance tests can be challenging due to
the wide range of tools and technologies now available. A decision-making framework for
implementing technology in sport (159) suggests four reflective questions:
1. Would the information provided be helpful?
2. Can you trust the information provided?
3. Can you integrate, manage, and analyze the data provided effectively?
4. Can you implement the technology in your practice?
Selecting suitable technology involves ensuring device validity and reliability, considering
practicality and ease of use, and confirming relevance to athletes and their specific sport. This
comprehensive approach ensures that the assessments are accurate, relevant, and actionable,
supporting athlete performance and reduced risk of injury.
