5. The time from third- to ninth-step contact is determined to the nearest 0.01 second using the

6. Power in watts is calculated as the athlete’s weight (w) in newtons (lb × 4.45 or kg × 9.807)
times height (h) in meters (inches × 0.0254) from the third step to the ninth step divided by
the measured time interval (t) in seconds; P (watts) = (w × h) / t.
7. Repeat the test two more times with a 2- to 3-minute recovery period between each trial.

   13  VELOCITY-BASED TESTING (VBT)
Equipment
A commercial VBT device and the equipment required to perform the exercise in question (e.g.,
an inertial measurement unit [IMU], a linear position transducer, a depth-sensing camera
system, and a computer-vision enabled smartphone application)
An Olympic-style barbell with weight plates and locking collars; the weight should be
appropriate for the individual athlete being tested (in this case, a 1RM back squat will be the
exercise evaluated)
Personnel
One tester/recorder
Procedure
1. Place the VBT device in a suitable position based on the type of data collection. For example, a
linear position transducer will be placed under the barbell and tethered to the barbell, whereas
an IMU sensor will be affixed directly to the barbell.
