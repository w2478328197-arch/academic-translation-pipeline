It is important to note that the Valsalva maneuver is not
necessary for generation of intra-abdominal pressure. In the Valsalva
maneuver, the glottis is closed, thus keeping air from escaping the
lungs, and the muscles of the abdomen and rib cage contract,
creating rigid compartments of liquid in the lower torso and air in the
upper torso. An advantage of the Valsalva maneuver is that it
increases the rigidity of the entire torso, making it easier to support
heavy loads (20). For example, when lifting heavy loads in the back
squat exercise, many athletes use the Valsalva maneuver, particularly
when the trunk is most inclined forward, near the transition from the
eccentric movement phase to the concentric movement phase.
