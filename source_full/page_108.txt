FIGURE 2.1 A first-class lever (the forearm). Elbow extension against resistance (e.g., a
triceps extension exercise). O = fulcrum; FM = muscle force; FR = resistive force; MM =
moment arm of the muscle force; MR = moment arm of the resistive force. Mechanical
advantage = MM/MR = 5 cm/40 cm = 0.125, which, being less than 1.0, is a disadvantage
in the common sense.
fulcrum—The pivot point of a lever.
lever—A rigid or semirigid body that, when subjected to a force
whose line of action does not pass through its pivot point, exerts
force on any object impeding its tendency to rotate (figure 2.2).
