Procedures
1. Assume the standard push-up starting position with the hands shoulder-width apart and
elbows and body straight (figure 14.16a). The bottom position for the standard push-up is
when the chest makes contact with the recorder’s fist held vertically against the ground (not
shown in figure 14.16b).
2. A modified version establishes a starting position with the knees rather than the feet
contacting the ground; the knees should be flexed to 90 degrees with the ankles crossed
(figure 14.17a). There is no standard criterion for the bottom position of the modified push-up
(44), but it has been suggested that the torso should come in contact with a foam roller rather
than a fist (figure 14.17b).
3. For either version, repetitions that do not achieve the bottom position are not counted.
Note: Normative data for the push-up are presented in table 14.17 near the end of the chapter.
