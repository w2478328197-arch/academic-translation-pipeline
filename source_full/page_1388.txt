Downward movement: Quickly remove the hands from the medicine
ball and drop down. Contact the ground with hands slightly wider
than hip-width apart and elbows slightly flexed. Allow the chest to
almost touch the medicine ball by letting the elbows flex.
Upward movement: Immediately and explosively push up by fully
extending the elbows. Quickly place the palms on the medicine ball
and repeat the movement.
Note: When the upper body is at maximal height during the upward
movement, the hands should be higher than the medicine ball.
COMMON ERRORS
Not achieving sufficient tension in the arms to stop the
downward movement of the chest, which collapses onto
the medicine ball
Not achieving sufficient bilateral vertical force to land
with both hands onto the medicine ball in a balanced
fashion
