Polypeptide Hormone Interactions
Polypeptide hormones are composed of chains of amino acids and
include hormones such as GH and insulin. Due to their non-lipophilic
nature, polypeptide hormones cannot cross the cell membrane, and,
therefore, their effects are mediated by secondary messengers
within the cell. When these hormones bind to their membrane
receptor, they cause a conformational change that allows the
receptor to transmit the hormonal signal inside the cell. This
transmission initiates a cascade of signaling events that, among
others, can affect metabolic activities, influence DNA transcription, or
kick-start mRNA translation at the ribosome. For example, insulin
activates a pathway that results in glucose transporters (specifically
GLUT4) migrating from the cytosol to the cell membrane, enhancing
the cell’s uptake of glucose (45). This is crucial for managing blood
glucose levels and cellular substrate availability for ATP production.
