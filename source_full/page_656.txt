Eating Disorders: Management and Care
It is not the responsibility of the strength and conditioning professional to treat
or diagnose an eating disorder. It is his or her ethical responsibility to assist the
athlete in attaining the proper diagnosis from a qualified physician and treatment
from a qualified treatment team. Therefore, strength and conditioning
professionals should be mindful of the symptoms of each eating disorder as well
as signs of disordered eating. Keep in mind that abnormal eating patterns and
amenorrhea alone are not indicative of an eating disorder. A professional who is
experienced and qualified in diagnosing and treating eating disorders should be
contacted when an athlete’s behavior is concerning.
