  15   REVERSE SLED DRAG
Starting Position
Stand in front of the sled and attach the sled to the body via a
hip harness (not shown) or hold separate handles in each hand
(shown).
Regardless of the attachment method to the sled, lean
backward a little while maintaining a relatively extended hip
position, and keep the feet in front of the knees to increase the
ability to apply propulsive horizontal forces and accelerate the
sled.
It is advisable to start with the two feet positioned together to
maximize the ability to apply horizontal force to initiate
movement of the heavy sled.
Backward Pulling Phase
Initiate a stepping pattern that initially focuses on performing a
series of short, relatively fast steps, which will increase the
ability to apply horizontal forces to the ground.
While building up speed, increase the length of the steps and
further reduce ground contact times.
The ability to apply propulsive horizontal forces to the ground
can be optimized by ensuring the front foot is typically
positioned in front of the knee throughout the entire pulling
phase.
MAJOR MUSCLES INVOLVED
gluteus maximus, semimembranosus, semitendinosus,
biceps femoris, vastus lateralis, vastus intermedius, vastus
medialis, rectus femoris, gastrocnemius, soleus
