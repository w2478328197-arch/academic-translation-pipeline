   5  NORDIC HAMSTRING TEST
Equipment
A commercially available instrumented device for the measurement of eccentric hamstring
strength or a video recording device
A stable flooring surface on which the dynamometer should be placed
Personnel
One tester/recorder
Procedure
1. Set up the instrumented device on a stable surface. The athlete should kneel on the testing
device and place the ankles under the hooks (within which force cells are housed to quantify
force). The athlete should be careful to only contact the hooks with the back of the ankles and
to not push off the board with the toes in order to gain leverage. If not using an instrumented
device but using videography instead, the camera (or mobile phone) should be placed in the
sagittal view and fixed using a tripod or similar and positioned so that the full range of motion
is recorded. The knees of the athlete should be a 90-degree angle of flexion relative to the
lower leg and the rest of the body (figure 14.6a). Care should be taken to ensure that the
athlete does not flex forward at the hips, because occasionally athletes will do this in order to
reduce the eccentric loading on the hamstrings.
2. The athlete should then lean forward under control (i.e., controlled acceleration) by extending
the knees, increasing the knee angle, and maintaining a straight line from the knees through
the shoulders (figure 14.6b).
3. After lowering to the ground in this fashion, the athlete may then use the hands to push back
up to the starting position. Typically, 3 to 6 sets of this test are performed.
