Emphasis Training Models
Emphasis training models, as shown in figure 22.5, contain aspects of both
parallel and sequential models of periodization (35, 37, 38). Similar to parallel
models, multiple training factors are targeted simultaneously but with varying
degrees of emphasis over time (38). Over time the degree of focus on the
individual training is shifted in a sequential manner that aligns with the periodized
training plan. Generally, when this model is implemented the training emphasis is
rotated every two weeks to optimize performance capacity (108). When varying
the emphasis some training factors will be undertaken with training loads that
stimulate adaptations, while some training factors are undertaken with training
loads that are used to maintain the athlete’s current state (38, 108). Additionally,
some training factors will be targeted with a minimal training dose, which would
result in a degree of detraining.
This training model is ideally suited for intermediate to advanced athletes who
compete in individual or team sports and have congested competitive schedules
(38). Examples of the use of this model can be seen in several individual sports,
such as track cycling (70), sprinting (27), and mixed martial arts (19). This model
has been also recommended for team-sport athletes (32) as well as tactical
athletes.
