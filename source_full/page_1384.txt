  49   VIKING THROW FOR HEIGHT
Intensity level: Low
Direction: Vertical
Equipment: Medicine or plyometric ball weighing 10 to 50 pounds
(5-23 kg)
Starting position: Start in an upright stance with the medicine ball
held in straight arms in front of the middle of the body.
Preparatory movement: Initiate a countermovement by performing a
deep and rapid squat action, keeping the ball held in straight arms.
Arm action: From the base of the countermovement, perform a rapid
triple extension of the hips, knees, and ankles to drive the ball
vertically. As the body rises, continue the upward momentum of the
ball so that it is released and thrown as high as possible at the top
of the triple extension.
COMMON ERRORS
Not optimizing leg extension and transferring power to
the upper body, reducing the impulse to the medicine
ball
Releasing the ball too early (before full leg and trunk
extension and shoulder flexion), reducing the impulse to
the medicine ball
