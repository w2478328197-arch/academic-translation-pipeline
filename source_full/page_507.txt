A clear understanding of similarities and differences between
mental states can be useful. It is important to note that several
nuances of anxiety exist and can include general anxiety disorder,
social anxiety, panic disorders, and phobias. Anxiety often presents
as a circular pattern of thoughts and feelings of heightened
importance known as ruminations and worry. Some predisposing
factors for anxiety include large social and schedule adjustments, a
biological family history of anxiety, negative life events, early trauma,
and shyness in childhood (6, 62). Lists of signs and symptoms of
anxiety, depression, and stress in athletes are in table 9.1. These
manifest in varied somatic, cognitive, and behavioral symptoms.
Often maladaptive coping and perceptions of impending outcomes or
doom fill the individual with urgency, overwhelming feelings of
dread, and focus on negative consequences. By the end of this
section, strength and conditioning professionals will be able to
identify signs and symptoms of anxiety, depression, and stress;
recognize the appropriate means of support; and develop an
appreciation of the impact of mental health on performance and
injury risk.
