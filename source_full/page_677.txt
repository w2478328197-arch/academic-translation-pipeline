Athletic Performance
Initially, researchers examining the ergogenicity of exogenously
administered anabolic steroids were unable to see any significant
performance effects (74, 201). Consequently, the scientific and
medical community suggested that anabolic steroids had little
influence on athletic performance. This, however, was contrary to
anecdotal reports emanating from gyms and training centers that
showed that athletes experienced large improvements in strength in
response to using anabolic steroids. On further examination of the
initial studies, several methodological flaws became apparent.
Several of these studies used physiological doses, in contrast to the
suprapharmacologic doses that are typically taken by athletes self-
administering androgens. In essence, these subjects were shutting
down their own endogenous production and replacing it with an
exogenous anabolic steroid. Another flaw in some of these
