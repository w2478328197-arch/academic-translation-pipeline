  22   POWER SKIP
Intensity level: Low
Direction: Vertical and horizontal
Starting position: One leg is lifted to approximately 90 degrees of hip
and knee flexion with the ankle dorsiflexed.
Arm action: Double arm
Preparatory movement: Begin with a countermovement on one leg.
Upward movement: Drive up and forward on one leg. Move the
flexed, non-jumping leg up and into greater hip and knee flexion
while jumping. Both arms should be used to assist with the upward
movement.
Downward movement: Land in the starting position on the same leg.
Immediately repeat the skip with the opposite leg.
Note: Emphasis is on high-quality ground contact and leg drive as
athletes determine the effectiveness of the skip. This movement
should be more vertical and with a faster ground contact than the
higher intensity linear bounding drills listed later in the chapter.
COMMON ERRORS
Not attacking the ground with significant intensity to
accelerate the center of mass forward in subsequent
takeoff; this influences the subsequent flight phases and
landings
Coordination errors in the double-arm action, which
detracts from the forward momentum achieved
