Note: When performing the mid-thigh pull the bar should not be
placed at the mid-thigh position (i.e., midpoint between the iliac
crest and the middle of the patella) because this will result in the
athlete being in the wrong position (e.g., excessive torso lean). Due
to the anthropometric differences (i.e., limb lengths) between
athletes, the bar position will not be the same for all athletes. As
such the coach should ensure that each athlete’s individualized
power position is used when performing this lift.
Upward Movement Phase
The upward movement phase mirrors the movements used when
performing the second pull in the power clean (or power snatch if
using a snatch grip).
Rapidly extend the knees, hips, and ankles, (Note: it is
important that the heels stay in contact with the floor for as
long as possible in order to maximize force transference to the
barbell.)
Keep the bar as close to the body as possible.
Keep the back neutral and the elbows pointing out to the sides.
Keep the shoulders over the bar and the elbows extended as
long as possible.
When the lower body joints reach full extension, rapidly shrug
the shoulders upwards with the elbows still fully extended and
pointing to the sides.
Downward Movement Phase
During the downward movement phase, the barbell is returned to
the blocks or rack and the starting position should be reestablished.
It is important that the start position is reestablished prior to each
repetition performed in a given set.
In a controlled manner while maintaining an upright trunk
position lower the barbell back to the starting position.
While lowering the barbell it is important that the trunk remains
rigid (i.e., in a neutral spine position).
