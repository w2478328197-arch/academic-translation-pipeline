condition is when a strength and conditioning professional knew or
should have known that a student-athlete had been diagnosed with
sickle cell trait but did not take proactive measures to ensure that
the athlete was placed in a conditioning setting with the proper
work-to-rest ratio to target the health condition that was separate
from the athletes without a previous sickle cell trait diagnosis. As a
result of the failure to modify the work-to-rest ratio of the athlete
with sickle cell trait and by requiring the athlete to train under the
same work-to-rest ratio guidelines as the athletes without any sickle
cell trait diagnosis, the athlete with sickle cell trait suffered either
significant organ damage or died as a result of performing a
conditioning workout. The strength and conditioning professional’s
duty was to train the athlete, who had been diagnosed with sickle
cell trait, within the parameters that ensured the health and safety
of the athlete with sickle cell trait, and the failure to do so caused
the injury or death of the athlete. In this example, the strength and
conditioning professional did not act with the required standard of
care and proximately caused a foreseeable damage or injury;
therefore, the actions of the strength and conditioning professional
coupled with the failure to act (i.e., modifying the work-to-rest ratio
of an athlete with sickle cell trait) were negligent. The foregoing
issue of negligence was litigated in a court of law where a jury found
the respondent entity liable and awarded damages to the petitioner
(24). Even though the issues of negligence can be decided in a trial
by jury, some cases reach a settlement agreement before being
litigated in a court of law, and some actions are dismissed by a
presiding judge through summary judgment. Summary judgment is
a judgment granted on a claim about which there is no genuine
issue of material fact and on which the movant is entitled to prevail
as a matter of law (14).
