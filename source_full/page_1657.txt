most activities involve both closed and open kinetic chain
movements. In sprinting, for example, while one lower extremity is
on the ground (closed kinetic chain), the other is in the air (open
kinetic chain), which means that both types of movement can occur
simultaneously. In some situations, an open chain exercise may
therefore be an equally appropriate choice. Some examples of upper
extremity open kinetic chain activities include the biceps curl and
bench press exercises.
