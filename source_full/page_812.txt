3. The distance from the center of the star to the touch position is measured.
4. The starting direction and support leg are chosen randomly. Three trials are performed for
each condition and averaged.
5. A 15-second rest is allowed between each reach.
6. Trials are discarded if the athlete does not touch the line, lifts the stance foot from the center
grid, loses balance, or does not maintain start and return positions for 1 full second.
7. Athletes should be given a minimum of four practice trials before being tested (103).
8. It has been suggested that testing the anteromedial, medial, and posteromedial positions is
sufficient for most situations (66).
