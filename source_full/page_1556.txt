Training Sequence
The order in which resistance and aerobic endurance training
sessions are completed may affect adaptations because the acute
physiological responses for resistance and aerobic endurance
training are distinct to each form of training (34) and may influence
the quality and quantity of training sessions completed on the same
day. Findings have been mixed on the effects of concurrent training
sequence on strength and V̇ O2max (25, 32, 45, 46). As a general
guide, however, the training sequence should be varied throughout
the phases of the training year and reflect the highest priority (e.g.,
maximizing strength or improving aerobic endurance) for each
training cycle; the most important training goal should be trained
first within a given workout while the athlete is fresh (125).
