Overtraining Considerations
Not all types of overtraining are identical.
Strength is well-protected.
Speed and power may be more sensitive to overtraining.
Psychological variables may be effective markers.
Hormonal variables indicate stress, not OT.
Orthopedic problems present different OTS.
Autonomic nervous system activity responds rapidly to OT.
Muscle fiber maladaptation can follow.
Overtraining Avoidance Strategies
Use active and passive recovery methods during training.
Allow one or more days of routine training abstinence to enhance recovery.
Rest days are critical.
Limit excessively long training sessions and too many sessions in a day.
Exercise caution with excessive training volumes and large increases in
training volume (>30% increases).
Prior exposure to stressful training enhances training stress tolerance.
Variation in the training stimulus is critical, and use of periodized training is
important.
Avoid excessive training to failure.
Coordinate resistance exercise with other stressors (e.g., sport training and
other conditioning).
Incorporate recovery strategies (e.g., sleep, diet, mental skills).
Appropriate diet for training (e.g., adequate Kcal, protein ingestion, glycogen
repletion, sufficient vitamins and minerals, adequate fluid intake).
Dietary supplements may help (e.g., recovery drinks, protein and
carbohydrate supplements, creatine, oral ATP).
Monitor outside stressors.
Monitor internal and external training loads.
Incorporate an athlete monitoring program.
