UI may be due to the imbalance of contraction strength and an
increase in abdominal pressure that results in deformation of the
connective tissue (99, 107). Programs should include prevention,
lifestyle modifications, pelvic floor strengthening, biofeedback, and
pharmacological interventions. Female athletes should be taught
how to properly engage their pelvic floor muscles and abdominal
wall muscles, and in some cases may need to see a physical
therapist that specializes in PFD. With proper education and pelvic
floor treatment, females can recover from UI (46, 55).
How Can Females Improve Their Health
Outcomes?
To help females improve their health outcomes, strength and conditioning
professionals should do the following:
Recommend working with appropriate medical staff to provide resources and
services as needed when they have identified signs and symptoms of
concerns that increase risk of health issues
Encourage females to seek services from a mental health provider when
initial symptoms of mental health disorders arise
Educate females on the appropriate amount and quality of sleep needed and
the important role it plays in the body’s recovery and for decreasing risk of
injury
Provide information to females about the variety of impairments that can
occur in the pelvic floor and encourage females to seek a specialized physical
therapist in PFD when signs and symptoms occur
