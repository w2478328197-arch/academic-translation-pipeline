Repetition Maximum Continuum
Figure 18.10 represents how RM ranges have been historically associated with
training goals. In general, relatively heavy loads should be used if the goal is
maximal strength, whereas light loads should be used to develop muscular
endurance. To state this another way, low xRMs appear to have the greatest
effect on maximal strength and high xRMs seem to result in better muscular
endurance improvements (65, 106, 141). In contrast to strength and muscular
endurance, a spectrum of RM loads may be used to develop muscle hypertrophy.
