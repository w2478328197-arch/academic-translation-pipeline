Designer Anabolic Steroid Control Act of
2014 (DASCA)
In the past few decades, the United States Congress enacted laws regulating
anabolic steroids. The first law was the Anabolic Steroid Control Act of 1990. This
was updated in 2004. Ten years later, this was updated again as the Designer
Anabolic Steroid Control Act of 2014 (DASCA). Noteworthy items about DASCA
include the following:
Congress felt that the laws before DASCA failed to sufficiently curtail the
misuse of anabolic steroids.
Through DASCA, Congress targeted those who would circumvent the Anabolic
Steroid Controlled Act of 2004 by manufacturing anabolic steroids with
slightly different chemical structures from those expressly controlled but that
are intended to cause the same potentially harmful effects.
DASCA amended the definition of “anabolic steroid” by adding 22 new
substances to the prior list.
