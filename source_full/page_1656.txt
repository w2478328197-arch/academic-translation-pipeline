FIGURE 23.5 Closed kinetic chain exercises: (a) squat exercise and (b) push-up exercise.
An open kinetic chain exercise uses a combination of
successively arranged joints in which the terminal joint is free to
move; open kinetic chain exercises allow for greater concentration
on an isolated joint or muscle (2). An example is the leg (knee)
extension exercise, during which the feet and lower legs are allowed
to move freely (figure 23.6). The leg extension allows greater
concentration on the quadriceps at the knee joint; in comparison,
the squat, which also uses the quadriceps muscles and knee joint,
relies on muscle activity on the hip and ankle joints as well. Although
