  17   ACCENTUATED ECCENTRICALLY
LOADED BENCH PRESS
Note: This exercise requires the use of weight releasers, which allow
additional load to be applied during the eccentric phase of the
movement. The spotting technique used is identical to that outlined
for the bench press in chapter 16.
Set-up
Prior to the athlete initiating the movement the spotter should
load the barbell with the appropriate weight and the weight
releasers are attached to the barbell.
It is important that the weight releasers are loaded correctly
and placed evenly on the barbell so that they release at the
completion of the downward movement phase without
disrupting the athlete’s normal movement pattern. As a general
rule, the weight releasers should be set so that they disengage
from the barbell approximately 2 to 4 inches (5-10 cm) before
the completion of the downward movement (51).
Starting Position
Lie in a supine position on a bench in the five-point body
contact position.
Place the body on the bench so that the eyes are below the
racked barbell.
Grasp the barbell with a closed, pronated grip slightly wider
than shoulder-width apart.
Signal the spotter for assistance in moving the barbell off the
supports.
Position the barbell over the chest with the elbows fully
extended.
All repetitions begin from this position.
