Volume assignments for power training are typically lower than those for
strength training to maximize the quality of exercise. This reduction in volume
results from fewer goal repetitions and lighter loads (figure 18.10 and table
18.11) rather than the recommended number of sets (30, 68, 105). The
common guideline is 3 to 5 sets (after warm-up) for power exercises included in
a trained athlete’s program (161).

Hypertrophy
It is generally accepted that higher training volumes are associated with
increases in muscular size (96, 139, 143). This is the result of both a moderate
to higher number of repetitions per set (8-12; see figure 18.10 and table 18.11)
and the commonly recommended 3 to 6 sets per exercise (91, 123). However,
there does not appear to be a meaningful difference in training-induced
