Plyometric Exercise and Aerobic Exercise
Many sports—such as basketball and soccer—have both an
anaerobic (i.e., power) and an aerobic component. Therefore,
multiple types of training must be combined to best prepare athletes
for these types of sports. Because aerobic exercise may have an
interfering effect on power production (17), it is advisable to perform
plyometric exercise before aerobic endurance training. The program
design variables do not change and should complement each other
to most effectively prepare these athletes for competition.
