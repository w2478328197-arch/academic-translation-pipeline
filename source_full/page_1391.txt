Starting position: Sit on the ground with the trunk at an
approximately 45-degree angle. The partner should be in front with
the medicine ball.
Preparatory movement: The partner throws the ball to the athlete’s
outstretched hands.
Drill action: Once the partner throws the ball, the athlete catches it
using both arms with minimal trunk extension and immediately
returns the ball to the partner.
Note: The force used to return the ball to the partner should come
predominantly from the abdominal muscles.
COMMON ERRORS
Extending at the hips to absorb the force of the ball,
increasing the amortization phase and reducing the
efficacy of the exercise
Not keeping the foot in contact with the ground,
reducing the stable base from which to exert force
