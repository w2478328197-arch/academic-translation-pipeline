Understanding Signaling Concepts
Different types of workouts trigger specific, complex cellular
endocrine signaling processes, thus highlighting the importance of
the selection for each of the five acute program variables and then
the total amount of work performed in a workout (70) for the
endocrine response. Deoxyribonucleic acid (DNA) primarily directs
protein synthesis, but the regulation and expression of DNA affect
many other cellular functions and the synthesis of various molecules
essential for life. Hormonal signaling processes begin when glands
release hormones during, or in anticipation of, a resistance exercise
workout. These hormones then bind to specific receptors in muscle
cells and other cells. This binding initiates a cascade of responses,

have genomic and non-genomic receptors, and this is where the
hormone signals its message to make proteins. Here is a simplified
breakdown of the signaling process for skeletal muscle:
1. Stimulating the muscle. Resistance exercise activates muscles,
prompting a hormonal response.
2. Hormonal response. Hormones are released and travel to
muscle cells.
3. Hormone–receptor interaction. Hormones bind to specific
receptors in the cell membrane of muscle cells (peptide
hormones) or inside the muscle cells (steroid hormones),
signaling the need for action inside the cell.
4. Intracellular signaling. This binding triggers a series of events
that lead to DNA signaling inside the nucleus.
