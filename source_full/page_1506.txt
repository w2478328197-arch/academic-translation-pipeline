The athlete starts in the center of a 5 × 5-yard or -meter box
and initiates an acceleration from a static position in a new
direction (i.e., forward, sideways, rearward) before decelerating
at the specific cone or zone. The athlete should begin
submaximally developing his or her ability to accelerate forward,
sideways, and rearward and practice a range of different
movements as outlined here:
Starting and accelerating forward (static initiation). Bilateral
to false step, two-point staggered, three-point stance,
prone, kneeling, supine, two-point staggered with torso
rotation
Starting and accelerating sideways (static initiation). Jab
step (side-step cut), split step (amplitude jump), shuffle
step, crossover or pivot, kneeling, two-point staggered with
torso rotation
Starting and accelerating rearward (static initiation). Pivot,
drop or pop turn, turns from nondominant and dominant
sides
