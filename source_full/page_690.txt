Adverse Effects
Because of insulin’s anabolic properties and its rumored ability to
potentiate the effects of growth hormone and insulin-like growth
factors, some individuals (primarily bodybuilders) take insulin by
injection. The use of insulin in this manner comes with serious
consequences. Possible outcomes in a previously healthy athlete
include immediate death or coma due to severe hypoglycemia (106).
Human Growth Hormone
Human growth hormone (HGH), a protein secreted from the
anterior pituitary gland, has several important physiological functions
that enhance its ergogenic effect. It is anabolic due to its stimulation
of bone and skeletal muscle growth, but it also has important
metabolic functions such as maintaining blood glucose levels,
increasing the uptake of glucose and amino acids into muscle cells
(67), and stimulating release of fatty acids from the fat cells.
The primary source of pharmacologic growth hormone is a
relatively complicated molecule from which it is synthetically derived
using recombinant DNA technology. However, until 1986, the only
source of the hormone was the pituitaries of human cadavers.
