Racks, barbells, and dumbbells should have a minimum of 3 feet
(0.9 m) of space between equipment to allow for movement without
danger to the athlete or spotter. If a spotter or multiple spotters are
needed (e.g., on a rack), providing additional space may be
required.
Traffic Flow
Traffic flow is heavily influenced by equipment placement. Many
facilities are one big room, and walkways can be created via
arrangement of the equipment. As mentioned, most racks or
machines are best placed lined up in a row running the length of the
facility. This usually creates two or three main walkways that should
be 5 to 7 feet (1.5-2.1 m) wide (2, 3, 13). For safety, all individuals
in the training facility should have clear access to an exit at all times.
Ensuring proper equipment and walkway spacing is important for
allowing clearance to move freely throughout the facility and for
ADA-required wheelchair access (3, 13).
Weight Room Pillars and Building
Structures
When placing equipment in facilities that contain supporting
structure, such as weight room pillars, columns, or other weight
room obstructions, logistical planning for training sessions and safety
factors should be prioritized. Due to the large size of many strength
and conditioning facilities, the inclusion of weight-supporting and
other building structures may be unavoidable. Strategically placing
training equipment with consideration of any vertical weight room
pillars or horizontal structural beams and designating specific
