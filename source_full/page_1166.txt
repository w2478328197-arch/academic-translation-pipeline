Lower the barbell under control to reduce the movement of the
weight releasers.
The weight releasers will disengage from the barbell about 2 to
4 inches (5-10 cm) before touching the chest at approximately
nipple level.
Keep the wrists stiff and the forearms perpendicular to the floor
and parallel to each other.
Maintain the five-point body contact position.
Upward Movement Phase
Push the barbell upward and very slightly backward as fast as
possible until the elbows are fully extended.
Keep the wrists stiff and the forearms perpendicular to the floor
and parallel to each other.
Maintain the five-point body contact position.
Do not arch the back or raise the chest to meet the bar.
At the end of the set, signal the spotter for assistance in racking
the bar.
Keep a grip on the bar until it is racked.

Starting Position
Stand facing the free end of the barbell, then squat down to
grasp the end of the barbell sleeve with both hands (not
shown) and pick it up.
After standing, press the free end of the barbell up to position it
in front of the midline of the body approximately level with the
eyes.
Stand with the feet approximately shoulder-width apart and
knees slightly flexed.
