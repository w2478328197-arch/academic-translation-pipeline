The body weight should be shifted to the balls of the feet but
with the heels remaining in contact with the floor.
Brace the trunk with the head and spine in a neutral position
(4).
Rotate to the Left
Lower the bar by rotating to the left while also allowing the
torso, hips, and back foot (i.e., the foot opposite the direction of
the bar rotation) to rotate in the same direction of the barbell
(4) (somewhat more than what is shown).
Return to the Starting Position
Using a forceful and rapid motion, rotate the bar back to the
starting position while keeping the torso and hips aligned with a
neutral head position.
During this motion keep the arms extended and in front of the
torso throughout the rotational elevation of the barbell while
returning to the starting position (4).

torso, hips, and back foot (i.e., the foot opposite the direction of
the bar rotation) to rotate in the same direction of the barbell
(4) (somewhat more than what is shown).
