FACILITY DESIGN, LAYOUT,

McMahon, MEd
The authors would like to acknowledge the significant contributions of Andrea Hudy,
Michael Greenwood, and Lori Greenwood to this chapter.
After completing this chapter, you will be able to
identify the aspects and phases of new strength and
conditioning facility design;
identify the aspects of updating or renovating an existing
strength and conditioning facility and transitioning an existing
building or space for strength and conditioning use;
explain how to assess the needs of an athletic program to
design a facility that is well suited to these needs, including the
planning of training spaces to accommodate various size
groups of athletes and coaches;
explain how to design specific facility features, including
supervision location, access, ceiling height, flooring,
environmental factors, electrical service, mirrors, and storage;
explain how to arrange equipment in organized groups,
creating better traffic flow and enhancing safety throughout the
