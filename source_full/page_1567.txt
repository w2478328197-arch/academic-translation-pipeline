Ending Position (Finish)
The knees should be fully extended with the torso leaning
slightly backward.
Arms are flexed at the elbows and the handle is at the abdomen
just below the rib cage.
Recovery is the movement from the finish position back to the start
in the reverse order of drive phase events—that is, extension of the
arms first before flexion at the hips (to move the upper body
forward), then flexion of the knees back to the start position.
COMMON ERRORS
Using arms and legs at the same time or pulling only
with arms—the legs provide the majority of the power in
the drive phase; the drive phase sequence is knees, hips,
then arms.
The handle going up and over the knees on the way back
or the chain hitting the machine in a whipping motion,
which is often due to the recovery sequence being out of
sync; the recovery sequence is the opposite of the drive
phase, so arms, hips then knees
