Stand on one leg with one leg flexed 90 degrees at the hip and
knee to ensure the thigh is parallel with the ground. The ankle
is neutral or dorsiflexed, which emphasizes front-side mechanics
and a positive shin angle. The contralateral arm is held with the
elbow flexed to <90 degrees and with the hand around the
height of the chin.
The foot of the standing leg is flat on the ground, and the
ipsilateral arm is relaxed by the side of the body.
The trunk is upright with the head up and eyes looking forward.
Movement Phase
While maintaining an upright trunk with the head up, the
athlete simultaneously drives the standing leg up and the flexed
leg down, emphasizing rapid switching of the limbs.
The contralateral arm movement matches the speed of the leg
movement.
The landing of the previously flexed limb on the ground and the
standing limb to the flexed position should occur at the same
time. Pause in this position (initial position) before repeating for
a desired number of repetitions.
Technique Tips
Ensure an upright trunk and head with no leaning back or
excessive lumbar flexion.
Emphasize rapid leg and arm switching, avoiding excessively
high thigh and knee lifts (>90 degrees).
Progressions
Rather than perform with a pause between repetitions, this drill
can progress to performing repeatedly for a desired number of
repetitions on the spot (e.g., 2 or 3) before pausing.
A further progression would be to add a mini-hop forward
between repetitions but still emphasizing the rapid limb,
switching to establish a more rhythmic pattern. Trunk stability
