On consistent activation, type IIx fibers change into a more
oxidative form along the continuum (i.e., to an intermediate fiber
type IIax and then to a IIa) (26). Any change in the muscle fiber
type continuum and associated MHCs occurs in the early stages of a
resistance training program. In an early study, Staron and colleagues
(158) examined the effects of a high-intensity resistance training
protocol (multiple sets of the squat, leg press, and knee extension
exercises using 6RM to 12RM loads and 2-min rest periods)
performed by men and women two times per week for eight weeks.
They reported a significant decrease in the type IIx percentage in
women after just two weeks of training (four workouts) and in the
men after four weeks of training (eight workouts). Over the eight-
week training program, type IIx fiber types decreased from
approximately 18% to about 7% of the total muscle fibers in both
men and women. Analysis of the MHCs showed that in this early
phase of training, the proportion of IIx MHCs shifted toward IIa
MHCs. Interestingly, detraining has the opposite effect, resulting in
an increase in type IIx fibers and a reduction in type IIa fibers (132),
