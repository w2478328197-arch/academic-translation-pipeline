Multiple layers of clothing and protective equipment contribute to
sweat losses and dehydration risk in hockey players (14), while
intentional dehydration and other unsafe weight loss practices can
increase dehydration risk in wrestlers (170). In addition, it is
important to note that a very wide range of fluid losses through
sweat exists. For instance, National Basketball Association players
lost from 1.0 to 4.6 liters (33.8-155.5 fl oz; 4.2-19.4 cups) and had a
mean loss of 2.2 ± 0.8 liters (74.4 ± 27.1 fl oz; 9.3 ± 3.4 cups) of
sweat over the course of a 40-minute game in which the average
playing time was 21 ± 8 minutes (120). To reduce the risk of
dehydration and heat stress, athletes should avoid exercising under
extremely hot, humid conditions and should adequately replace fluid
losses from prolonged exertion.
Preventing Dehydration
Given the negative effects associated with dehydration, athletes
should try to prevent water weight losses exceeding 2% of body
weight while also restoring electrolytes lost through sweat (3, 139).
