CHAPTER 5
ADAPTATIONS TO
ANAEROBIC TRAINING
Brandon Roberts, PhD, and Sean Collins, PhD
The authors would like to acknowledge the significant contributions of Duncan French
and Nicholas A. Ratamess to this chapter.
After completing this chapter, you will be able to
differentiate between aerobic training adaptations and the
anatomical, physiological, and performance adaptations
following anaerobic training;
discuss the central and peripheral neural adaptations to
anaerobic training;
understand how manipulating the acute training variables of a
periodized program can alter bone, muscle structure, fiber
types, and connective tissue;
explain the acute and chronic effects of anaerobic training on
the endocrine system;
elucidate the chronic effects of anaerobic training on the
cardiovascular system;
understand the detrimental effects as a result of periods of
detraining; and
discuss how anaerobic training programs have the potential to
