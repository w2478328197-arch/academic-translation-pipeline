CHAPTER 24
OVERREACHING,
OVERTRAINING, AND
RECOVERY
Andrew C. Fry, PhD, and Bradley C. Nindl, PhD
After completing this chapter, you will be able to
define and differentiate overtraining and overreaching;
understand the difference between the process of overtraining
and the result of the overtraining syndrome;
recognize contributing factors to overtraining and overreaching;
understand the role of allostatic loading;
realize that not all decreases in performance are due to
overtraining;
know the physical performances most sensitive to overtraining
and overreaching;
appreciate the physiological, biomechanical, and psychological
responses and adaptations to overtraining and overreaching;
learn about potential strategies for avoiding performance
decrements due to overtraining; and
understand how functional overreaching can be a beneficial
part of an appropriately designed training program.
