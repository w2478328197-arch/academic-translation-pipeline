Patellofemoral Pain Syndrome
Patellofemoral pain syndrome (PFPS) is a broad term used for
pain arising from the patellofemoral joint, also known as runner’s
knee or jumper’s knee (24). Female athletes are more than two
times more likely to have incidences of PFPS compared to male
athletes (25). PFPS has been shown to be attributed to the hip
mechanics in female athletes before puberty, and modified
mechanics in the knee may be related to PFPS throughout
development in female athletes (60). Strength and conditioning
professionals should focus on lower extremity biomechanics, exercise
technique,

Trainers’ Association has a position statement that provides guidance
to practitioners on how to manage patellofemoral pain in athletes,
including use of bracing, taping, or other modifications (24).
Bone Stress Injuries
Bone stress injuries, including stress fractures, have regularly
been shown to occur at a higher rate in female athletes than in male
athletes (34, 153). The accumulation of repetitive stress, bone stress
injuries frequently occur in athletes (1, 153) and are considered
overuse injuries, where abnormal bone remodeling occurs (180).

workload, and though it is challenging to identify individual bone
stress tolerance, it is possible to generally monitor bone health and
