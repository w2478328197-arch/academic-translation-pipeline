FIGURE 3.6 The Krebs cycle. CoA = coenzyme A; FAD2+, FADH, FADH2 = flavin adenine
dinucleotide; GDP = guanine diphosphate; GTP = guanine triphosphate; NAD+, NADH =
nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide.
Also produced from the two pyruvate molecules subsequent to
the production of one molecule of glucose are six molecules of
NADH + H+ and two molecules of reduced flavin adenine
dinucleotide (FADH2). These molecules transport hydrogen atoms
to the ETC to be used to produce ATP from ADP (22, 109). The ETC
uses the NADH + H+ and FADH2 molecules to rephosphorylate ADP
to ATP (figure 3.7).
