Position the barbell on some boxes, anywhere from a full
squat position to standing almost completely upright.
Position the arms under and around the bar with the palms
facing up so that the bar now rests on the inner part of the
elbows and is held closer the upper abdominal region. It
might be easier to maintain this position by clasping the
hands and keeping them close to the chest (not shown).
2. Starting position from the floor
Position the barbell on the floor near the shins with a closed
overhand grip and then perform a semi-sumo squat deadlift.
Once the bar is above the knees, drop into a full semi-sumo
squat position and reposition the arms under and through
the bar so that the bar is on the inner part of the elbows
and contacting the upper abdominal region.
While maintaining the position of the bar on the inner elbows and
upper abdominals, forcibly push the feet into the ground and extend
the knees and hips, now standing upright with the bar kept in the
same relative position as liftoff.
Walking Phase
Adopt a walking movement pattern, initially focusing on
performing a series of short, relatively fast steps, which will
increase total body balance and stability.
When building up speed, increase the length of the steps and
further reduce ground contact times.
Note: Lean back a little during the walking phase so as not to lose
balance forward due to the barbell positioned in front of the center
of mass.
Ending Position
After completing the set distance, reset the bar on the boxes by
performing the eccentric phase of a full or partial squatting
motion, depending on the height.
