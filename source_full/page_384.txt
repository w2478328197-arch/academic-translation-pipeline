CHAPTER 7
AGE-RELATED DIFFERENCES
AND THEIR IMPLICATIONS
FOR RESISTANCE TRAINING
Rhodri S. Lloyd, PhD, Sylvia Moeskops, PhD, and
Avery D. Faigenbaum, EdD
After completing this chapter, you will be able to
understand the merits of adopting a long-term approach to the
development of athleticism for child and adolescent
populations;
evaluate the evidence regarding the safety, effectiveness, and
importance of resistance exercise for youth populations;
describe the effects of aging on musculoskeletal health and
performance;
explore the trainability of older adults and the benefits of
exercise, including resistance training in aging populations; and
explain why adaptations to resistance exercise vary greatly
among these distinct populations.
