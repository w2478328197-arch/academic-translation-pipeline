While the number of quality studies investigating the impact of
warm-up on performance is surprisingly low, more than 80% of the
published research has demonstrated a positive effect on
performance (37, 40). These performance improvements include
enhancement of endurance performance (both aerobic and
anaerobic), improvements in performance on physical tasks such as
jumping, and in actual sporting performance (37).
Whenever an analysis of the effects of warm-up is carried out, or
where recommendations as to the optimal form of warm-up are
required, it is crucial to consider that the direct effects will be related
to the structure and content of the warm-up, the characteristics of
the athletes concerned, and the nature of the subsequent activity
(37). As a result, while general findings can be identified and
recommendations made, a degree of specificity is required in any
scenario. Consequently, it is highly unlikely that a perfect warm-up
