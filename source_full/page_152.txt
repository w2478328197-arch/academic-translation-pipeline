Nautilus Sports/Medical Industries popularized the concept of
tailoring resistive torque through the range of joint motion by
creating an exercise machine that uses a cam of variable radius; this
changes the length of the moment arm through which the weight
stack acts (figure 2.14). The rationale was to provide more
resistance at points in the range of motion where the muscles could
exert greater torque, and less resistance where the muscles could
apply less torque. For the system to work as planned, however, the
athlete has to move at a constant, slow angular velocity, which is
difficult to do consistently. Also, cam-based machines frequently fail
to match normal human torque capability patterns (12).
