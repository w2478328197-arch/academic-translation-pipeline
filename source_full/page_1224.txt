Table 18.8 shows the relationship between a submaximal load—calculated as
a percentage of the 1RM—and the number of repetitions that can be performed
at that load. By definition, 100% of the 1RM allows the athlete to perform 1
repetition. As the percentage of the 1RM (i.e., the load lifted) decreases, the
athlete will be able to successfully complete more repetitions. However, strength
and conditioning professionals should be cautious because the number of
repetitions that can be performed at a given load may be specific to the exercise
as well as the individual (120, 134).
