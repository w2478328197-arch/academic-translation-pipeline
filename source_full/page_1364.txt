Upward movement: On landing, immediately jump forward as far as
possible with both feet.
Note: When stepping from the box, step straight out. Do not first
jump up or lower the center of gravity while stepping down, because
these adjustments will change the height from which the exercise is
performed. The time on the ground should be kept to a minimum;
there is an optimal relationship between ground contact and
resultant horizontal projection.
COMMON ERRORS
Not achieving an optimal forward-leaning angle through
landing (i.e., too vertical and the ground contact time is
increased or horizontal distance is lost, whereas leaning
too far forward reduces the ground reaction force
applied)
Not pushing maximally into the ground to optimize the
lower limb push for horizontal distance
