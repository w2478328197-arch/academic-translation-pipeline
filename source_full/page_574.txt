Strength athletes should consume 1.4 to 2.0 grams of protein per kilogram
body weight per day.
Ingest 20 to 40 grams (0.25-0.40 g of protein per kg of body mass) of high-
quality protein every 3 to 4 hours throughout the day.
Carbohydrate
Reduce intake of calories from added sugars.
Increase intake of vegetables (prepared without added salt or fat) and fruit
(prepared without added sugars).
Eat a variety of vegetables, including beans; peas; starchy; and dark-green,
red, and orange vegetables.
Consume at least half of all total grains as whole grains. Individuals who eat
refined grains should choose enriched grains.
Fats and Alcohol
Consume less than 10% of calories from saturated fats by replacing them
with unsaturated fats, particularly polyunsaturated fats.
If alcohol is consumed, it should be consumed in moderation—up to one
drink per day for women and two drinks per day for men—and only by adults
of legal drinking age. Pregnant women should avoid alcohol, and
breastfeeding women should be cautious about their intake if they choose to
drink and speak to their healthcare provider. Alcohol should be avoided in the
time period after exercise because it reduces muscle protein synthesis (124).
