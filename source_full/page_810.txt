Flat running surface with start and finish lines a specified distance apart (e.g., 40 yd [37 m];
10 m, 20 m, 40 m), with at least 20 yards (18 m) after the finish line for deceleration
Where multiple timing gates are available, set these up to determine additional split times, in
addition to the start and finish lines (e.g., start line, 10 yd [9 m], 20 yd [18 m], finish line at 40
yd [37 m]). This enables greater insight into acceleration capacities in addition to the maximal
speed.
Personnel
One timer/recorder
Procedure
1. Have the athlete warm up and dynamically stretch for several minutes.
2. Allow at least two practice runs at submaximal speed.
3. The athlete assumes a starting position using a three- or four-point stance.
4. On an auditory signal, the athlete sprints the specified distance at maximal speed.
5. The best split times of two trials are recorded to the nearest 0.1 second.
6. Allow at least 2 minutes of active recovery or rest between trials.
Note: Normative data for the 10-meter (11 yd), 20-meter (22 yd), 40-meter (44 yd), and 40-yard
(37 m) sprint are presented in table 14.33 near the end of the chapter.
Balance and Stability
  31   BALANCE ERROR SCORING SYSTEM (BESS)
Equipment
Foam balance pad
Stopwatch
Personnel
One timer/recorder
Procedure
1. The six positions of the BESS are shown in figure 14.26.
2. The three stance positions are double-leg stance with feet together, single-leg stance on the
nondominant foot with contralateral leg in approximately 90 degrees of flexion, and tandem
stance with the dominant foot in front of the nondominant foot (128). The test is conducted on
a firm surface and on a soft surface.
3. The stances are held for 20 seconds with eyes closed and hands on hips for each condition.
4. Athletes are told to keep as steady as possible, and if they lose balance, they attempt to regain
their initial position as quickly as possible.
5. Errors include opening eyes; lifting the hands from the hips; touchdown of nonstance foot;
step, hop, or other movement of the stance foot or feet; lifting the forefeet or heel; moving
the hip into more than 30 degrees of hip flexion or abduction; or remaining out of position for
more than 5 seconds.
6. The error scores from the BESS test are summed into a single score.
Note: Normative data for the BESS are presented in table 14.34 near the end of the chapter.
