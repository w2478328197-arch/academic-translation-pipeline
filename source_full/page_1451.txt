Foot Position
Foot contact is on the balls of the feet.
Feet are placed perpendicular to movement (parallel or slight hip external
rotation).
Feet stay close to the ground.
Foot placement is wider than the knees; increased width increases stability
and lowers COM.
The feet should not cross. (In a diagonal or faster shuffle, the feet may
cross using a crossover step to help with the directional change or provide
extra propulsion to increase velocity [21].)
Leg Action
Take short steps, driving more from the leg farthest (i.e., pushing the medial
aspect of foot) from the intended direction of travel (i.e., COM moves
medially relative to center of pressure at toe-off), with the other leg acting
as a pivot, allowing the COM to travel medially and assist the shuffle step
(COM is now over center of pressure).
Leg action is close to the ground in preparation for ground contact.
COM Travel
COM is projected medially or laterally, with minimal vertical projection and
oscillation.
The legs remain in a loaded athletic position and the trunk remains vertical.
