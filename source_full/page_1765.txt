Conclusion
Designing a strength and conditioning facility is an extensive process
that involves a significant amount of planning. The process begins
with the formation of a committee that will contribute to the design
and construction of the facility. The next step involves assessing the
specific needs of the strength and conditioning department. This is
the phase in which the number and experience level of athletes
being 
trained, 
preferences 
from 
strength 
and 
conditioning
professionals and staff, scheduling, and equipment needs should be
determined. After these determinations have been made, the
process of designing the facility and arranging the equipment can
begin. Care should be taken and guidelines followed in arranging
and spacing equipment to optimize safety, function, and traffic flow.
After the design process is complete, the facility and the
equipment must be properly maintained and cared for to ensure safe
training and protect financial investments. Regular maintenance and
cleaning of equipment and surfaces are vital for the longevity of the
facility, in addition to health and safety. Cleaning and maintenance
should focus the most on high-traffic areas and equipment that is
used more often. This will help to prevent the spread of infections
and disease among the facility users as well as promote proper
hygiene and maintain the appearance of the environment. It is also
suggested that once the facility is operational, key stakeholders meet
periodically to discuss outcomes and plans for future changes (9).
