Muscle Fiber Types
Skeletal muscles are composed of muscle fibers that have markedly
different morphological and physiological characteristics (37). These
differences have led to different classification systems, with the most
familiar including the terms slow-twitch and fast-twitch muscle
fibers. Because a motor unit innervates muscle fibers that are
metabolically and functionally similar to one another, motor units
have also been historically designated using this nomenclature. A
fast-twitch motor unit is one that develops force and relaxes rapidly,
thus exhibiting a shorter twitch time. Slow-twitch motor units, in
contrast, develop force and relax slowly and have a longer twitch
time. General differences between these types of motor units can be
found in figure 1.9.
Regarding muscle fiber classification, histochemical staining for
myosin ATPase content was also used for years to classify myofibers
as type I (slow-twitch), type IIa (fast-twitch), and type IIx (fast-
twitch). Other methods used for myofiber classification involve single
fiber isolation techniques to quantify the presence of different
myosin heavy chain isoforms and immunohistochemical staining for
myosin heavy chain isoforms (33). The nomenclature for both
techniques is akin to that associated with the myosin ATPase
histology methods.
