When the lower body joints reach full extension, rapidly shrug
the shoulders upward with the elbows still fully extended and
pointing to the sides.
As the shoulders reach their highest elevation, flex the elbows
to begin pulling the body under the bar.
Due to the explosive nature of this phase, the torso is erect or
slightly hyperextended, the head may tilt slightly back, and the
heels may lose contact with the floor.
Downward Movement Phase
During the downward movement phase, the barbell is returned to
the blocks or rack and the starting position should be reestablished.
It is important that the start position is reestablished before each
repetition performed in a given set.
Flex at the knee and hip while lowering the barbell to the
blocks.
Maintain a neutral back and ensure the elbows are pointing to
the sides.
Avoid bouncing the barbell off the blocks.
Note: The mid-thigh pull can be performed with loads greater than
the loads that are used when performing the power clean or power
snatch. In fact, it is not uncommon for athletes to be able to perform
the mid-thigh pulls with as much as 140% of the 1RM.
