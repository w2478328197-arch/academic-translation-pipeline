Starting position: Start in a half-kneeling position, with the kneeling
knee directly under the hip of the supporting leg and the hip and
knee of the stance leg flexed to approximately 90 degrees with the
hip, knee, and toe aligned. The trunk should be upright, facing the
wall or partner approximately 10 feet (3 m) away (not shown). Raise
the ball above the head with the elbows extended.
Preparatory movement: Begin with a countermovement in the trunk
as well as the arms, cocking the arms (i.e., moving the arms slightly
backward, from the shoulder, before the actual throw).
Arm action: Using both arms, throw the ball to the wall or partner by
flexing the trunk and extending the shoulders, maintaining a straight
arm position. When the wall or partner returns the ball, return to the
starting position, and immediately repeat the movement.
COMMON ERRORS
Overextending the trunk in the start position,
detrimentally increasing the involvement of the lumbar
spine
Producing a cross-body throwing motion (either from not
having the ball directly overhead or by finishing with the
hands toward one hip); this produces trunk rotation,
which may increase the distance but changes the
exercise from an isolated sagittal plane movement
