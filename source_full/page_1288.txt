The following are classifications and examples of complex training
based on Cormier and colleagues (22):
Contrast training involves alternating high-load exercises
and high-velocity exercises in contrast pairs in a set-by-set
fashion with sufficient inter-set rest to avoid fatigue-induced
interference. An example is an American football lineman
alternating sets of heavy bench presses with sets of clap push-
ups.
Ascending complex training involves sets of a high-velocity
exercise followed by sets of a contrasting high-force exercise.
An example is a weightlifter doing 2 sets of maximal-effort box
jumps before performing mid-thigh power cleans.
Descending sets require multiple high-force exercises to be
completed before the execution of high-velocity exercises. An
example is a thrower doing 3 sets of 2 repetitions of heavy
back squats followed by 3 sets of 4 repetitions of overhead
medicine ball throws.
