Heat Acclimatization
The physiologic responses to aerobic exercise depend on the
environmental conditions in which an athlete is exercising.
Consequently, high environmental temperatures and humidity cause
significant alterations to heart rate, oxygen uptake, minute
ventilation, and blood lactate responses and, ultimately, performance
due to interactions between the environmental conditions and an
athlete’s thermoregulatory systems (117). If these environmental
conditions are hot and dry or warm/hot and humid and produce a
sufficient level of heat stress, performance can be impaired due to a
cascade of thermoregulatory responses including an increase in
sweat rate and blood flow to the skin to facilitate greater heat
transfer to the environment. Such a process is in competition with
increasing skeletal muscle blood flow to meet the metabolic
