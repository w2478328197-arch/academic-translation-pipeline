Change-of-Direction Ability
Similar to the progressions of plyometric activities based on intensity
and difficulty of each drill, closed-skill COD drills can progress from
beginner to intermediate to advanced levels based on the physical
loading demands that have been discussed in this chapter
(regulation of angle and intensity, which affect mechanical loading)
(36). Table 20.5 presents an example of a progression of drills for
COD ability (both rapid COD and those 
associated 
with
maneuverability), as well as a progression plan for agility drills. The
physical loading during agility drills will be higher than beginner COD
drills. Therefore, table 20.5 provides practitioners with a series of
drills and a guideline for safely and effectively advancing athletes so
they can build on their physical and technical attributes as they
improve.
