Gollnick and colleagues (67) and others (8, 72, 118) have
reported that blood lactate concentrations normally return to pre-
exercise values within an hour after activity, depending on the
duration and intensity of exercise, training status, and type of
recovery (i.e., passive versus active). Light activity during the
postexercise period has been shown to increase lactate clearance
rates (55, 67, 72, 80, 118). For example, an active recovery
following a 200-yard (182.9 m) maximal-effort swim resulted in the
greatest lactate clearance in comparison to a passive recovery in
