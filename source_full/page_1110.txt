Similar to how resistance is applied with the use of chains, the
use of bands requires the strength and conditioning professional to
determine how much load is provided by the free weight and how
much is provided from the bands throughout the range of movement
(8). Specifically, the strength and conditioning professional must
determine the load of the band at the bottom and top portions of
the movement and create an average of these two loads. Based on
the recommendations of Baker (8), an athlete who wants to train at
a 5RM load of 150 kilograms (331 lb) with bands would subtract the
average of the two band positions (i.e., top and bottom) and reduce
the load on the bar by that amount. So if the band exerts a zero
resistance load at the bottom of the movement and a load of 26.6
kilograms (58.6 lb) at the top position (i.e., lockout in the bench
press), based on the length of the band achieved as it is stretched,
the athlete would subtract 13.3 kilograms (29.3 lb) from the total
weight on the bar without the bands to get 136 to 137 kilograms
(300-302 lb).
