the cutting techniques performed at the turning point during
preplanned and Y-shaped agility tasks. Alternative cuts include
the following:
Crossover cut. The athlete positions the plant foot on the
same side (ipsilateral) of the new direction (or sometimes
medially across the pelvic midline) and then crosses the
opposite leg (contralateral) in front of the body for the new
step in the new direction, accelerating in the same direction
of the push-off leg (35).
Split step cut. The athlete performs a small jump (amplitude
jump) before push-off, landing with both feet greater than or
equal to shoulder-width apart (not shown; it would come
after the second photo), and then, on landing, the
contralateral limb is used for push-off into the intended
direction of travel (35).
