  48   SINGLE-ARM REVERSE THROW
Intensity level: Low
Direction: Backward
Equipment: Small medicine or plyometric ball weighing 2 to 30
pounds (1-14 kg); partner
Starting position: Start in a tall-kneeling position, with the kneeling
knee directly under the hip and the trunk upright, facing away from
a partner who is standing approximately 1 to 2 feet (30-61 cm)
behind the athlete. The partner starts with the ball. The athlete
starts with the throwing arm raised above the head with the elbow
extended.
Preparatory movement: The partner throws the ball over the
athlete’s head so that its trajectory takes it an arm’s length in front
of the athlete, traveling toward the opposite knee to the raised arm.
Arm action: The athlete tracks the ball in a diagonal pattern with the
throwing arm and catches it with an overhand grip before it hits the
ground. Once caught, the athlete keeps the elbow extended while
flexing the shoulder in the diagonal pattern, moving to a release
