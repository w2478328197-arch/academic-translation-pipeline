  33   BOX HOP
Intensity level: Low
Direction: Vertical and slightly horizontal
Equipment: Plyometric box, 6 to 42 inches (15-107 cm) high
Starting position: Facing the plyometric box, get into a comfortable,
upright stance on one foot. The non-jumping leg is held in a
stationary position with the hip and knee flexed during the exercise.
Arm action: Double arm
Preparatory movement: Begin with a countermovement.
Upward movement: Forcefully triple extend and hop onto the top of
the box.
Downward movement: Land on the same foot in a triple flexion
position (hip, knee, and ankle flexed and stable), step down from
the box, and repeat.
Note: Although significant force may be required to hop onto boxes
in excess of 30 inches (76 cm), this is a low-intensity drill because
the athlete is accelerating against gravity and lands with less force
than his or her body weight because of this.
COMMON ERRORS
Focusing on landing on the box and flexing hips and
knees early, thus reducing vertical power; the aim should
be to land on the box because of powerful extension into
the ground, maximizing the forward and upward
movement of the body
Not coordinating arm actions with the forward
movement of the body; powerful shoulder flexion should
be simultaneous with the lower body jumping action
