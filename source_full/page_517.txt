Personal Factors
The psychological impact of an injury on an athlete can be very
specific to each individual. Strength and conditioning professionals
can better understand how athletes will respond to injury by
understanding these key personal factors:
History of injury. Is this injury acute or chronic? Has the
athlete experienced this injury before?
Athletic identity. Does the athlete highly identify as an athlete
and look to others for acknowledgment of that role? According
to a 2022 study, almost 40% of student-athletes have a high
athletic identity, strongly serving as a foundation of their self-
worth (2).
Personality. Is the athlete naturally confident and outgoing or
introverted? Do they chronically worry about what they cannot
accomplish or perform?
Pain tolerance. How well does the athlete tolerate discomfort
during training and rehabilitation?
Coping skills. How has the athlete managed stressful situations
in the past? Do they engage in positive (e.g., regular physical
activity or relaxation strategies) or negative (e.g., risky
activities or avoidant behaviors) coping strategies?
Age and sex. Does the athlete have specific concerns due to
their age (e.g., transportation) or sex (biological differences
such as energy availability or hormonal differences)?
