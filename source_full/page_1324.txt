Upward movement: Jump from the outside of the first hurdle to the
outside of the second hurdle, keeping the hips and shoulders facing
the direction of travel.
Downward movement: In flight, dorsiflex the ankles in preparation
for an active flat-foot landing. Immediately on landing on the outside
of the second hurdle, change direction and jump diagonally over the
second hurdle to the outside of the third hurdle. Continue jumping
over all the hurdles.
Note: Hurdle height can significantly alter the intensity and in-flight
body position of the athlete. The athlete should be as extended as
possible to clear the hurdle. (Note: The second photo shows the
athlete clearing the hurdle too high relative to the hurdle height and
with excessive knee flexion.) Correct execution will result in the hips
and knees being as stiff as possible on landing to optimize ground
contact. Intensity of the drill can be significantly increased by
performing hops with only one leg.
COMMON ERRORS
Not adjusting the body angle coming into a landing to
keep the center of mass going in the desired direction of
the next jump; this significantly increases ground
contact time
Not “attacking the ground” from a dorsiflexed ankle
position, which increases ground contact time
