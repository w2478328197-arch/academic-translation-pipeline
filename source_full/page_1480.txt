Ground
contact time
(GCT)
The total time the foot is in
contact with the ground during
the COD (length of the plant
phase, main execution plant
foot contact)
Shorter ground contact times are
generally associated with faster COD
performance and indicate the athlete is
spending less time performing the COD
(if the athlete deflects their COM at the
same angle). Insight into GCT can
establish whether the athlete is
improving their COD ability irrespective
of total time. GCT can be measured
using a built-in force platform, contact
mat, or verified using 2D analysis.
Exit velocity
The horizontal velocity of
the athlete during the initial
step out of the plant of the
COD
Considered the first step of
reacceleration from plant
foot to foot strike
Unlike the situation for total time, using
a high-speed video camera or 3D motion
analysis to assess the velocity of the
athlete (at the hip or COM) can give a
direct measure of the ability to change
direction. Improved exit velocity with the
same or faster ground contact times
implies greater RFD relevant to
improving COD ability.
Entry velocity
Velocity of the athlete before
the plant phase; can be
measured over several steps or
as the last step entering the
plant phase of the COD
Entry velocity is a strong determinant of
COD completion time, and the ability to
enter the COD with high velocity can be
advantageous for COD actions. Insight
into entry velocity can be used to
establish whether any pacing or self-
regulatory strategies are adopted.
Assessed using the above methods for
