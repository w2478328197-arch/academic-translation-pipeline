FIGURE 8.1 Phases of the menstrual cycle and the concomitant changes in estrogen and
progesterone.
Reprinted by permission from W.L. Kenney, J.H. Wilmore, and D.L. Costill, “Sex Differences in Sport and Exercise,” in
Physiology of Sport and Exercise, 9th ed. (Human Kinetics, 2024).
After an acute bout of resistance exercise, increased estradiol
concentrations have been reported (94, 158). This response may
occur due to increased activity of the enzyme that converts
testosterone to estrogen (estradiol) (129), which may also contribute
to increases in circulating testosterone (101). Because of the
beneficial effects of estrogen on muscle, it is important for females
to maintain normal menstrual cycle function in order to maximize
their adaptations to training (37) and reduce the risks associated
with low estradiol levels (173).
