1. Assume an upright stance with feet hip-width apart and arms to
the side (note: this preparatory position is not shown).
2. Shift the body weight to the left foot and at the same time drop
the hips back and down, reaching the right leg behind the body
and as far as possible to the opposite (right) side. The result is
a fully extended rear (right) knee and a contralateral (opposite)
lateral reach of the other (left) arm.
3. Push back to the beginning position and repeat on the other
side.
4. For a more advanced exercise, the movement can be initiated
by a lateral hop.
MUSCLES AFFECTED
quadriceps, gluteus maximus, gluteus medius, hip
adductors, hamstrings
COMMON ERRORS
Taking a step that is too small or too long
Losing postural integrity during the movement
