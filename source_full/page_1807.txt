Guideline 3.1 in NSCA Strength and Conditioning Professional
Standards and Guidelines states the following:
Strength and Conditioning activities should be planned, and the
requisite number of qualified staff should be available such that
recommended guidelines for minimum average floor space
allowed per participant (100 ft2), professional-to-participant
ratios (1:10 junior high school, 1:15 high school, 1:20 college),
and number of participants per barbell or training station are
achieved during peak usage times. (30, p. 10)
The foregoing guidelines should be modified for increased levels
of supervision if the exercises being implemented involve complex
movements.
Supervision was cited in an estimated 80% of court cases in
which athletic injuries occurred (5). Inadequate instruction or
supervision is often a main cause. All athletes should be constantly
supervised, which requires the physical presence of a qualified
strength and conditioning professional for overseeing all activities
(20, 21, 26). To provide proper supervision, supervisor stations need
to be located where a clear view of the facility and athletes is
possible (30). Suggested staff-to-athlete ratios should also be
adhered to, but these can vary depending on the type of training.
For instance, if a group of athletes is performing a machine circuit
training workout, they do not need as much supervision as athletes
who are lifting with free weights. Also, athletes with a low training
age need more supervision than athletes who are experienced in the
weight room.
