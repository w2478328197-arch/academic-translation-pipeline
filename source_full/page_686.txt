MK-677 was associated with adverse effects on cholesterol,
testosterone, liver function markers, and bone mineral density in a
25-year-old resistance-trained male (37). Somapacitan, an FDA-
approved peptide for use in adults with GH deficiency, reduces trunk
fat and increases lean body mass in adults with GH deficiency (124).
However, it is notable that chronic (~20 weeks) administration of
peptides such as hexarelin have no effects on body composition
despite causing profound increases in GH release in healthy adults
(178). Other peptides, such as the GLP-1 receptor agonists
liraglutide and semaglutide, have demonstrated strong efficacy for
weight loss in individuals with overweight or obesity (169, 223), and
despite a lack of evidence for efficacy among individuals without
excess body fat, these weight loss effects may be intriguing to
recreational users for cosmetic reasons. Table 12.4 lists some

Despite the promise of short-term safety and efficacy displayed by
several SARMs in initial clinical trials among healthy individuals,
substantial work is still necessary to understand the short- and long-
term risks associated with SARM and peptide use, understanding
that each SARM and peptide is structurally different and is therefore
likely to produce different primary and secondary (or side) effects.
Moreover, and as noted earlier, in many cases, SARMs available on
the market may include other potentially unsafe, nondisclosed
substances or may not contain the disclosed SARM at all (212);
therefore, risks of SARM or peptide use may be amplified among
recreational users. Figure 12.3 lists the short-term changes in lean
