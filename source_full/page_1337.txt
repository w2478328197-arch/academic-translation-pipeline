  23   LATERAL SKIP
Intensity level: Medium
Direction: Vertical and lateral
Starting position: One leg is lifted to approximately 90 degrees of hip
and knee flexion with the ankle dorsiflexed. This leg should be
opposite to the direction of movement (i.e., if the skip is to the right,
the left leg should be raised).
Arm action: Reciprocal (as one leg is lifted, the opposite arm is
lifted)
Preparatory movement: Drive the lifted leg forcefully toward the
ground and away from the direction of movement so that it contacts
the ground outside of the base of support.
Upward movement: The lateral drive of the downward leg will cause
the body to move up and sideways away from the foot contact. As
this happens, the other leg should flex at the hip and knee in flight.
The drive leg typically takes a small adjustment step to aid stability
and rhythm in the action.
