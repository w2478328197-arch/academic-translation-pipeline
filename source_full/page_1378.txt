Direction: Upward
Equipment: Medicine or plyometric ball weighing 5 to 40 pounds (2-
18 kg); partner; plyometric box 12 to 42 inches (30-107 cm) high
Starting position: Lie supine on the ground with elbows extended
and both shoulders in approximately 90 degrees of flexion; the head
should be near the base of the box. The partner should be on the
box with the medicine ball held above the athlete’s arms.
Preparatory movement: None
Arm action: When the partner drops the ball, catch it using both
arms and immediately throw the ball back up to the partner.
COMMON ERRORS
Catching the ball too high and absorbing the impact
force, increasing the amortization phase
Releasing the medicine ball too early, reducing the
potential vertical impulse to the ball
