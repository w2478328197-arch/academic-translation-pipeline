Chronic Adaptations of the Acute
Cardiovascular Response to Anaerobic
Exercise
Chronic resistance training reduces the cardiovascular response to
an acute bout of resistance exercise of a given absolute intensity or
workload. Short-term studies have shown that resistance training is
associated with reductions in the acute increases in heart rate, blood
pressure, and double product caused by submaximal resistance
exercise but may promote an increase in the pressor response to
maximal or isometric exercise (119, 142). In addition, male
bodybuilders have been found to have lower systolic and diastolic
blood pressure and heart rates during sets of 50% to 100% of 1RM
performed to momentary muscular failure compared to both
sedentary and lesser-trained men (56). Finally, oxygen extraction is
generally not improved with resistance training using heavy loads
and low volume. It is enhanced to a greater extent with continuous
aerobic exercise or with a resistance training program using high
volume and short rest periods or among populations with low
baseline cardiorespiratory fitness (97, 119a).
