Test Selection
When evaluating tests for high levels of validity and reliability, the
strength and conditioning professional must rely on her or his
knowledge and practical experience with the sport. The strength and
conditioning professional must consider sport specificity (e.g.,
metabolic energy systems, biomechanical movement patterns),
athlete experience, training status, age, and environmental factors
when selecting tests.
When considering test selection, it is imperative to classify the
exercises to be tested. The categorization of exercises facilitates the
creation of a comprehensive testing battery that accurately evaluates
an athlete’s capabilities across various movement patterns and
muscle groups. Understanding the classification system assists
strength and conditioning professionals in designing targeted
training programs tailored to individual needs and goals. The
classification of exercises for assessment typically encompasses
several key dimensions of specificity:
Movement patterns. Exercises can be classified based on the
primary movement patterns they involve, such as pushing,
pulling, squatting, hinging, rotating, and stabilizing (7, 8). Each
